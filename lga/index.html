Terminal B: PreCheck waits have varied significantly. On Bluesky, Tue Mar 24, 1:24 PM, PreCheck was approximately 30 minutes during lunchtime. Earlier that same day, at Twitter, Tue Mar 24, 11:44 AM, a traveler waited 1 hour 37 minutes in the regular security line (6:37 AM to 8:14 AM). By contrast, Bluesky, Tue Mar 24, 11:58 AM noted that while the standard line wrapped across the entire terminal, the PreCheck line was only about 10 people deep. On Twitter, Tue Mar 24, 12:59 PM, a traveler reported 5 minutes through TSA PreCheck at 12:05 PM.

Non-terminal-specific reports: Across all LGA terminals, Twitter, Tue Mar 24, 2:05 PM reported average wait times of 39 minutes (typical: 6 minutes, a difference of +33 minutes). An earlier system-wide report from Twitter, Tue Mar 24, 12:03 PM showed average waits of 67 minutes (typical: 8 minutes, a difference of +59 minutes). On Bluesky, Tue Mar 24, 10:03 AM, a traveler reported 2 hours in the TSA line. On Bluesky, Sun Mar 22, 6:20 PM, waits reached 2 hours with the line wrapping around the entire terminal; notably, the PreCheck line was reportedly no better. On Bluesky, Sun Mar 22, 1:51 PM, PreCheck at the American Airlines terminal took 20 minutes. On Bluesky, Sun Mar 22, 4:37 AM, a 6 AM flight resulted in a 2–3 hour line; that traveler also noted the PreCheck line was "psychotic."

Arrival recommendation: TSA PreCheck is strongly recommended—it has consistently shown 5–30 minute waits versus 1.5–3 hour waits in regular lines. If you have PreCheck, plan to arrive 90 minutes to 2 hours before your flight. Without PreCheck, arrive 3 hours early, as regular security lines have exceeded 2 hours multiple times this week. Terminal B PreCheck appears slightly faster than system-wide averages, but do not rely on this alone.
