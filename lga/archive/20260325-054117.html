A traveler reported a 29-minute wait in the regular security line at LaGuardia this morning (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:46 PM). On Monday, a traveler reported spending 3 hours in line for TSA at LaGuardia (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 2:41 PM).

Earlier reports from Sunday showed highly variable conditions. One traveler spent 2 hours getting through security, with the line wrapping around the entire terminal before reaching the traditional security line start; the TSA PreCheck line was similarly congested (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 6:20 PM). In contrast, another traveler at the American Airlines terminal reported 20 minutes from ride-share drop-off through TSA PreCheck (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 1:51 PM). An early-morning traveler (6:00 AM flight) described a 2–3 hour line, noting that even the PreCheck line was exceptionally crowded; their PreCheck status also did not appear on their ticket (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 4:37 AM).

Recommendation: LGA wait times have been highly inconsistent in recent days—ranging from no wait to multi-hour delays. If you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, you may see faster processing, but recent reports suggest even PreCheck lines have been slow. Plan to arrive 2–3 hours early for flights, especially during peak morning hours. Check real-time updates closer to your departure time, as conditions appear to vary significantly by terminal and time of day.
