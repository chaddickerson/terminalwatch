TSA Watch: LGA

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 11:18 AM ET — 8 reports across 3 days

Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 11:17 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 10:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:57 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

Tuesday morning at LGA showed sharp variations by terminal and security line type. Terminal B experienced severe congestion, with reports of lines snaking through the entire terminal (Tue Mar 24, 10:06 AM), and at least one traveler reporting a 2-hour wait (Tue Mar 24, 10:03 AM). However, Terminal C PreCheck showed dramatically better conditions with no wait at 9:30 AM (Tue Mar 24, 09:40 AM), and another traveler reported a quick 20-minute journey from car to security in the American Airlines terminal on Sunday afternoon (Sun Mar 22, 1:51 PM).

The data reveals a stark PreCheck advantage when available. While standard security lines have hit 2–3 hours during early morning departures (Sun Mar 22, 4:37 AM reported "2–3 hour" waits), PreCheck users have experienced waits as short as 20 minutes (Sun Mar 22, 1:51 PM). One traveler noted that even the PreCheck line was congested on Sunday evening (Sun Mar 22, 06:20 PM reported a 2-hour wait despite holding PreCheck status), suggesting occasional system-wide backups.

Recommendation: If flying during morning hours, plan to arrive 3 hours early for standard security, particularly in Terminal B. PreCheck holders should plan for at least 45 minutes to 1 hour as a buffer, though you may clear faster. Check which terminal your airline uses—Terminal C has shown lighter loads than Terminal B on recent reports.
