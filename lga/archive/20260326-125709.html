Terminal B

Early this morning, the TSA Precheck line at Terminal B showed significant congestion, though specific wait times weren't provided (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:43 AM). At 4:30 a.m., the security line snaked through the terminal multiple times (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:32 AM). Yesterday evening, a traveler cleared security in less than 10 minutes after entering the line at 7:56 p.m., with ICE agents conducting ID checks (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:41 PM).

Terminal C

Today at 5:50 a.m., Terminal C experienced chaotic conditions with lines extending to parking garages. Among PreCheck travelers, regular PreCheck moved fastest at 30 minutes, TSA touchless was next fastest, and CLEAR with PreCheck was notably slower (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:12 AM). This pattern was confirmed by another traveler who cleared the TSA Pre touchless line in 29 minutes at 5:30 a.m. and noted it moved significantly faster than the CLEAR line, even for those with both credentials (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:44 AM). Yesterday just after 5:30 p.m., the digital ID line took approximately 3 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:40 PM).

General Reports

Wait times across LaGuardia have been severe today. As of 8:26 a.m., the airport averaged 43 minutes across all lines—37 minutes longer than typical (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 8:26 AM). Multiple travelers reported waiting 3 hours in security lines this morning (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:45 AM, Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:19 AM, Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:49 AM). One traveler noted even PreCheck lines exceeded 2.5 hours (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:06 AM). A TSA officer described severe staffing shortages and extended lines since 4 a.m. (Bluesky - Thu Mar 26, 8:21 AM). Yesterday, the contrast between PreCheck and regular lines was stark: PreCheck wait times were only 15–20 minutes while regular lines exceeded 3 hours (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:18 PM). On Wednesday evening, waits reached 83 minutes across all lines (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:04 PM). Earlier in the week, a traveler spent 2.5 hours in the regular line (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 2:38 PM), and lines wrapped around the terminal twice on Monday (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 10:43 AM).

Recommendation

Plan to arrive at least 3–4 hours early for domestic flights today—significantly more than the standard recommendation. If you have TSA PreCheck, expect 30 minutes to 2.5 hours depending on which screening lane you use; at Terminal C specifically, regular PreCheck has moved notably faster than CLEAR-combined lanes. Avoid CLEAR alone if possible, as it has been slower than standard PreCheck. Regular (non-PreCheck) passengers should plan for 3+ hours.
