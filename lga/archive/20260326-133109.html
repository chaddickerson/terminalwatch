Airport Update: LGA

Terminal C
Terminal C saw relatively short wait times today. A traveler cleared security in just 5 minutes at 8:00 a.m. (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 8:09 AM). Earlier this morning around 5:50 a.m., the terminal experienced chaotic conditions with lines extending to parking garages, but PreCheck moved faster than expected. Regular PreCheck was the fastest option, followed by TSA Touchless, while CLEAR with PreCheck was notably slower at approximately 29 minutes (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:44 AM). Another traveler reported being through PreCheck in 30 minutes during the same early morning surge (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:12 AM).

Terminal A
Terminal A reported exceptionally short wait times today. A traveler described clearing security with one of the shortest waits of their entire life at 7:53 a.m. (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:53 AM).

Terminal B
Terminal B experienced severe congestion this morning. A traveler required just under 2 hours to clear security, waiting from 3:00 a.m. to 4:55 a.m. (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:49 AM). At 5:30 a.m., the PreCheck line alone showed significant backup (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:43 AM), with a traveler noting at 4:30 a.m. that the line snaked through the terminal four times (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:32 AM). On the evening of March 25, one traveler cleared security in less than 10 minutes at 7:56 p.m., with ICE agents conducting ID checks (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:41 PM). Earlier on March 24, PreCheck waited approximately 30 minutes during lunchtime, while the standard security line wrapped across the entire terminal (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 1:24 PM).

General Reports (All Terminals)
Today's airport-wide conditions have been significantly worse than normal. Average wait times across all security lines reached 43 minutes by 8:26 a.m., compared to a typical 6 minutes—a 37-minute increase (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 8:26 AM). One traveler reported experiencing the longest queues they had ever seen and expressed urgency in obtaining Global Entry (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 8:50 AM). A TSA officer described battling three-hour lines since 4:00 a.m., with staffing challenges cited as a contributing factor (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 8:21 AM). Multiple travelers reported waits of 3 hours in standard lines this morning (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:45 AM; Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:19 AM). One traveler with PreCheck questioned whether the wait would exceed 2.5 hours even with expedited screening (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:06 AM). Staff reported at least 3-hour waits for newly arriving passengers at 6:49 a.m. (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:49 AM). By 6:24 a.m., average wait times across all lines had reached 58 minutes, up from a typical 10 minutes (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:24 AM). On March 25, a traveler waited 2 hours 30 minutes in the regular (non-PreCheck) line, while staff handed out water bottles and lollipops to those waiting (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 2:38 PM). That same day, PreCheck lines waited approximately 15–20 minutes while regular lines exceeded 3 hours (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:18 PM). On March 24, one traveler with CLEAR reported no significant delays (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 2:45 AM), and another with PreCheck noted the standard line wrapped across Terminal B while the PreCheck line held only about 10 people (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 11:58 AM).

Recommendation
Plan to arrive significantly earlier than usual. Today's conditions show waits reaching 3 hours in standard lines, with some PreCheck waits extending beyond 2 hours. Terminal A appears to be moving much faster than Terminals B and C. If you have PreCheck or CLEAR, use them—but note that PreCheck (especially regular PreCheck or TSA Touchless) is moving faster than CLEAR alone in Terminal C. If flying from Terminal B today, add at least 2–3 hours to your arrival window for standard screening; PreCheck travelers should plan for 1–2 hours. Terminal C travelers should plan for 30 minutes to 1 hour with PreCheck based on this morning's data. Standard lines remain severely backed up across all terminals.
