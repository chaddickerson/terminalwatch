Terminal B: Today, TSA lines at Terminal B experienced significant congestion during early morning hours. A traveler reported needing to avoid the terminal between 5–8 AM, noting waits of approximately 3 hours (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:10 AM). TSA PreCheck was running 38 minutes at approximately 4:30 AM, with the line appearing roughly 10 minutes longer by 5 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:21 AM).

Terminal C: Terminal C showed much faster service today. A traveler cleared TSA in just 10 minutes at 6:31 AM using the touchless ID terminal (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:31 AM).

General reports today noted a traveler experienced 1 hour 10 minutes in a standard security line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:17 AM), and another reported 1.5–2 hours to get through security, advising early arrival (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:09 AM). The TSA recheck line was notably faster at just 30 seconds (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:06 AM).

Additional context from recent days: A traveler reported 29 minutes without PreCheck or CLEAR on Tuesday morning (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:46 PM). On Sunday, a traveler spent 2 hours in security with lines wrapping around the entire terminal, and noted that the TSA PreCheck line was similarly congested (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 6:20 PM). Earlier on Sunday, a traveler with TSA PreCheck at the American Airlines terminal (Terminal B) cleared security in 20 minutes (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 1:51 PM).

Recommendation: Today's data shows Terminal C is significantly faster than Terminal B. If possible, use Terminal C—a 10-minute wait versus 3+ hours elsewhere. If you must use Terminal B, arrive very early (well before 5 AM) or avoid the 5–8 AM window entirely. TSA PreCheck and CLEAR appear to offer modest time savings but do not guarantee short waits during peak periods; even PreCheck hit 38+ minutes in Terminal B this morning. Plan for 1–2 hours of buffer time for regular lines during morning hours.
