TSA Watch: LGA

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 01:35 PM ET — 11 reports across 3 days

Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 01:34 PM ET|Mar 24, 2026 12:37 PM ET|Mar 24, 2026 11:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 11:17 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 10:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:57 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

Security at LaGuardia is experiencing significantly longer waits than normal. The average across all terminals is currently 67 minutes compared to a typical 8 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 12:03 PM). Terminal B has been particularly affected, with standard security lines wrapping across the entire terminal (Tue Mar 24, 11:58 AM). Early morning has been especially congested, with one traveler reporting a 1 hour 37 minute wait starting at 6:37 AM (Tue Mar 24, 11:44 AM), and another describing a 2-3 hour line for a 6 AM departure (Sun Mar 22, 4:37 AM).

PreCheck is your best option if you have it. PreCheck waits have been substantially shorter—around 20-30 minutes during midday hours (Tue Mar 24, 12:59 PM and 1:24 PM), and as quick as 5 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 12:59 PM)—compared to standard line waits that have hit 2+ hours. However, PreCheck lines have also gotten backed up during peak times; one traveler noted the PreCheck line was "psychotic too" during an early morning departure (Sun Mar 22, 4:37 AM).

Arrive 2.5 to 3 hours early for domestic flights if you don't have PreCheck, and plan for at least 2 hours if you do. Standard lines are particularly severe in early morning (before 9 AM) and midday (around noon). If possible, shift your travel to off-peak times or apply for PreCheck now if you haven't already—it's making a measurable difference in your security experience at LGA.
