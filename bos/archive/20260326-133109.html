Airport Update: BOS

Terminal A: PreCheck was moving exceptionally fast today, with a traveler reporting only 4 minutes when arriving at 5:45 AM for a 7:45 AM flight, while the regular security line was "very manageable" (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:07 AM). Earlier in the week, the PreCheck line at Terminal A had no one waiting, while the regular PreCheck line held about 8 people (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:32 PM).

Terminal B: Security lines have been notably short this week. One traveler reported the TSA line took just 6 minutes 53 seconds on Tuesday morning (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 9:48 AM). Another noted the security line was shorter than the Dunkin' line on the same day (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:52 AM), and at 9:30 AM on Tuesday, security was essentially nonexistent (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:06 PM).

Today, PreCheck lines moved quickly while regular security showed a significant backlog. One traveler dropping off a passenger at 4 AM today reported PreCheck processed smoothly, but the regular security line had over 100 people waiting (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 8:54 AM).

Reports from earlier in the week indicated generally easy conditions across the airport. On Tuesday, there were no lines at TSA (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:14 PM). On Monday, one traveler was the second person in line for security (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 12:45 PM), and another reported wait times of around 5 minutes (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 10:53 AM).

Recommendation: Arrive at least 90 minutes early if you're using regular security today, as lines are currently backed up with over 100 people waiting. PreCheck travelers are experiencing much faster processing—arriving 1 hour early should be sufficient based on today's performance. If you have CLEAR, you should move through even faster. The gap between PreCheck and regular lines has widened significantly today, making PreCheck enrollment or CLEAR membership particularly valuable at this time.
