TSA Watch: BOS

Last updated: Mar 26, 2026 08:35 AM ET — 17 reports across 4 days

Mar 26: 7:36 AM|6:36 AM|6:02 AM

Terminal A

TSA PreCheck at Terminal A was 4 minutes early this morning (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:07 AM). A traveler who arrived at 5:45 a.m. for a 7:45 a.m. flight noted the normal security line also seemed very manageable. Earlier, around 8:45–9:00 a.m., TSA PreCheck had no one in line, while the regular PreCheck line had about 8 people (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:32 PM).

Terminal B

TSA wait times at Terminal B have been consistently short. This morning, security took 5 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:07 AM). On Tuesday, one traveler cleared security in 6 minutes 53 seconds and praised the friendly TSA staff (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 9:48 AM). The airport even had a longer line for Dunkin' than for TSA (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:52 AM). At 9:30 a.m., the security line was nonexistent (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:06 PM).

Terminal E

Terminal E staff encouraged most passengers to check bags, resulting in exceptionally fast TSA processing (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:07 PM).

General Reports

On Tuesday, there were no lines at TSA (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:14 PM), and TSA PreCheck wait times were around 5 minutes for travelers with PreCheck status across multiple visits (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 10:54 PM). A report from Monday noted that lines were normal and TSA officers were very nice, despite operating without pay during the shutdown (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 11:24 AM). Another traveler reported a 5-minute wait on a Monday visit (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 10:53 AM).

Arrival Recommendations

Boston Logan has maintained notably short wait times across terminals. For TSA PreCheck, arriving 2–2.5 hours before your flight should be more than sufficient; most recent reports show 5–6 minute waits. Regular security lines have also moved quickly, typically under 10 minutes based on recent reports. Terminal B appears particularly efficient. However, budget extra time for parking and ground transportation, as one traveler noted that getting to the airport was the real bottleneck, not TSA itself.
