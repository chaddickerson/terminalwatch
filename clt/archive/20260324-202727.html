TSA PreCheck continues to move exceptionally fast at CLT today. A traveler reported no wait in the TSA PreCheck line around 11 a.m. today. Another report noted that both regular and TSA PreCheck lines are operating at approximately 5 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:22 AM).

General reports today show remarkably light traffic across CLT. A traveler flying to Miami tomorrow described being pleasantly surprised by conditions (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:12 PM). Spring break season has not created significant delays—one traveler who visited Charlotte, Cincinnati, Chicago, and Orlando over the past three weeks reported all airports maintained wait times under 10–15 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:47 AM).

Yesterday, Monday Mar 23, saw mixed reports. However, numerous traveler reports from the same day contradicted this, with most describing smooth, quick passages. A traveler who transferred from a Munich flight reported clearing domestic TSA in under 30 minutes total (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 4:02 PM). One report noted that PreCheck was closed Friday night, though security moved faster than usual (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:13 AM).

Earlier reports from the weekend and previous week (Sunday Mar 22 through last week) consistently showed minimal wait times. A traveler flying EWR-CLT-EWR experienced under one minute security screening both ways with no TSA PreCheck line (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 6:20 PM). A brother's flight home Sunday night saw a 6-minute wait (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 2:42 PM). Multiple travelers reported sub-5-minute passages in recent weeks, with some exceeding 3 minutes.

Recommendation: Arrive 90 minutes to 2 hours before departure for domestic flights. If you have TSA PreCheck, 60–75 minutes should be sufficient based on today's data showing PreCheck lines at 5 minutes or less. Current conditions at CLT are notably faster than at comparable airports; however, the Monday alert suggests occasional spikes, so building in a moderate buffer remains prudent. If PreCheck is unavailable, plan for the upper end of the window.
