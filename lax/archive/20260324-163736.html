TSA Watch: LAX

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 11:40 AM ET — 20 reports across 2 days

Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 11:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 11:17 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 10:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:57 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

LAX security screening is currently moving quickly. As of early Tuesday morning (Tue Mar 24, 10:38 AM ET), travelers reported short wait times with all TSA stations fully staffed and operational. The earliest reports from Tuesday (Tue Mar 24, 5:47 AM ET and Tue Mar 24, 07:56 AM ET) showed waits of just 9-10 minutes in the pre-dawn hours, with one traveler completing screening in 10 minutes at 4:30 AM.

TSA PreCheck continues to offer significant advantages. Travelers with PreCheck reported waits as low as 2-4 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 11:09 PM ET and Mon Mar 23, 11:19 AM ET), compared to general security lines reaching 9 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 11:09 PM ET). This pattern held through Monday evening, when general security averaged 30-31 minutes during peak hours (Mon Mar 23, 9:41 PM ET and 7:41 PM ET), while PreCheck remained significantly faster.

Wait times vary by time of day. Early morning arrivals (before 5 AM) consistently show the shortest screening times, while Monday evening reports indicated peak congestion from 7-9 AM and 3-6 PM (Mon Mar 23, 9:31 PM ET). Off-peak hours like early morning and after 8 PM typically see 10-15 minute waits.

Recommendation: Arrive at LAX 90 minutes before departure if you have TSA PreCheck, or 2 hours if using general security. The current staffing levels support reasonable processing times, but account for parking, check-in, and the unpredictable Los Angeles airport traffic separate from the TSA line itself.
