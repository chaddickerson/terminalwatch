Another report noted essentially no line at the American terminal (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:42 AM).

General LAX reports today showed consistently fast processing. One traveler got through in 7 minutes curb to gate (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:30 PM). At 9:00 AM, another passenger reported nothing out of the ordinary and breezed through despite arriving three hours early (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:18 PM). Additional reports throughout the day noted 10-minute (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:24 PM) and 13-minute (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:20 AM) total times, plus zero waits in other instances (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:40 AM).

Recommendation: Today's reports suggest LAX is moving exceptionally well across most terminals. If you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, expect minimal waits—Terminal 7 and Bradley saw near-zero times this morning. Terminal 3 was notably slower at 30 minutes, so factor in extra time there if departing from that terminal. For regular security at other terminals, 10–15 minutes appears typical today. Arriving 90 minutes before domestic flights should be sufficient, though PreCheck holders can likely manage with less buffer time. Conditions can shift, so monitor updates closer to your departure.
