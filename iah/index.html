TSA Watch: IAH

Last updated: Mar 26, 2026 07:34 AM ET — 20 reports across 3 days

Mar 26: 6:36 AM|6:02 AM

Terminal A: Wait times today reached 232 minutes (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:49 AM), significantly above the typical 34 minutes. A reporter noted that only terminals A and E were open this morning, with 4-hour waits reported (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:06 AM).

Terminal E: Wait times today climbed to 232 minutes (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:49 AM), compared to a typical 25 minutes. TSA screening gates did not open on schedule this morning—they were still closed at 4:16 AM when they were supposed to open at 4:00 AM (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:16 AM).

Across all IAH terminals, average wait times spiked throughout the morning. At 2:36 AM today, the average was 31 minutes (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 2:36 AM), then jumped to 182 minutes by 5:24 AM (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:24 AM), and reached 264 minutes by 6:24 AM today (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:24 AM). A traveler who arrived 4 hours before their flight reported that only 2 TSA terminals were open, with the security line extending all the way down to the subway and nearly the full length of the underground tunnel (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:24 AM). Another traveler waited 2 hours just to reach the TSA checkpoint area (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:50 AM).

Recommendation: Today's conditions at IAH are severely disrupted due to limited checkpoint staffing. If traveling from Terminal A or Terminal E today, plan to arrive at least 5–6 hours before your flight for originating flights. The typical advantage of PreCheck or CLEAR is unlikely to help significantly given the bottleneck at checkpoint openings. Travelers should monitor real-time updates before heading to the airport and consider rescheduling if possible.
