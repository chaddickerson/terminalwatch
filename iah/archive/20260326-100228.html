TSA Watch: IAH

Last updated: Mar 26, 2026 05:58 AM ET — 7 reports across 3 days

Today, TSA wait times at IAH reached critical levels during early morning hours. A traveler who arrived 4 hours before their flight reported that only 2 TSA terminals were open, with the security line stretching down to the subway and through nearly the entire underground tunnel (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:24 AM). At 3:39 AM, the TSA line extended downstairs with a long walk to reach the end (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:47 AM). By 5:50 AM today, one traveler had already waited 2 hours just to reach the TSA checkpoint area (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:50 AM).

Yesterday at IAH, a traveler completed TSA screening in 1 hour from start to finish (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 12:36 AM), though another report from Wednesday afternoon described a 3-hour wait (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:16 PM). Earlier in the week, a traveler monitoring conditions anticipated 4–6 hour waits and chose to reschedule their trip (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 12:59 PM).

Recommendation: Today's reports suggest arriving significantly earlier than the standard 2 hours—consider 3–4 hours before your flight given the severe bottlenecks and limited terminals in operation. Early morning flights (before 6 AM) have experienced the longest delays. If you have flexibility, consider flying later in the day or rescheduling.
