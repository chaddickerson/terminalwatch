Airport Update: DEN

TSA PreCheck at Denver International Airport moved exceptionally fast today, with one traveler reporting zero wait (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:33 PM). Another PreCheck traveler with touchless technology got through in just 2 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:12 PM). CLEAR users experienced similarly short times, with one traveler reporting less than 3 minutes end-to-end (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:07 PM).

Non-terminal-specific reports show wait times have remained consistently short across DEN. One traveler reported 10 minutes for standard security on their return flight to Denver (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 12:00 AM). On Wednesday afternoon, standard security lines were moving in 3–7 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 2:31 PM). Earlier in the week, real-time average wait times held at 10 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:26 PM), and over the prior two weeks, security wait times averaged 12 minutes, with travelers never waiting longer than 35 minutes and often getting through in under 10 minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 1:43 PM). One traveler on a light flight got through in less than 1 minute (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:12 AM).

Arrive 15 minutes early for standard security and flights with flexible schedules. If you have PreCheck or CLEAR, 10 minutes early should be sufficient given the consistently short processing times. Even during moderate Spring Break travel volumes, standard lines remained under 15 minutes, so current conditions support these shorter buffers.
