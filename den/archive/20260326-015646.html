Touchless TSA PreCheck was exceptionally fast today, with one traveler clearing security in just 2 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:12 PM).

At the East Security Checkpoint, mid-afternoon wait times today were very light: standard lanes reported 2–6 minutes and TSA PreCheck 1–5 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:25 PM). However, one traveler reported a significantly longer wait of 90 minutes in standard security on an incoming flight from San Juan earlier that same day (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:17 PM).

General reports from today indicate very manageable conditions. Multiple travelers described lines as "super quick" and "no line at all," with several noting waits under 10 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:18 PM; Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:25 PM). One traveler who recently flew through Denver and several other major airports reported consistent waits of about 1 minute across all cities (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:34 PM).

Reports from yesterday (Tuesday) show similarly quick processing. CLEAR users experienced notably faster speeds, with one traveler reporting completion in less than 3 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:07 PM). The West checkpoint opened in just 2 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:47 AM), while standard lines remained under 15 minutes despite higher spring break volumes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 11:51 AM).

Recommendation: Based on current conditions, plan to arrive 45 minutes to 1 hour before domestic departure. TSA PreCheck and CLEAR users can arrive closer to 30–45 minutes before departure, as these lanes consistently process travelers in 1–5 minutes. Standard lanes are running 2–15 minutes under normal conditions, though occasional longer waits have been reported; building in extra time on busy travel days (like spring break) is prudent.
