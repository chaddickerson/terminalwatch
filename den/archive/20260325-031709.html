PreCheck moved quickly today, taking just 4 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 04:34 PM). CLEAR passengers reported even faster service, clearing security in less than three minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 06:07 PM).

Regular security lines at DEN have been exceptionally short. One traveler encountered no line at all this evening at the normal (non-PreCheck) checkpoint, and noted staffing levels were high (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 08:48 PM). Earlier today, a traveler cleared the west gate checkpoint in just 2 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 07:47 AM). Other reports from today showed wait times of 5 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 02:27 PM) and 7 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 07:04 PM). The morning commute saw lines under 15 minutes despite spring break traffic, with one traveler at 7:47 a.m. noting TSA staff performed well (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 11:51 AM).

Yesterday, DEN security remained efficient. A real-time report showed 10 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 06:26 PM), and other travelers reported 5 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 01:29 PM) and 10–15 minutes (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 10:22 PM). Sunday morning brought a 15-minute wait with all lanes open (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 08:42 PM). One traveler over the weekend encountered 20–30 minutes at DEN (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 02:16 PM), though another experienced 4 hours each way on a DEN–LAX round trip this week (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 05:04 AM).

Arrival recommendation: Plan to arrive 15–20 minutes before security screening for regular lines and standard conditions. CLEAR and PreCheck users can arrive even closer to their screening time. DEN has been consistently fast, but spring break travel is ongoing, so allowing extra time during peak hours (morning and mid-afternoon) is prudent.
