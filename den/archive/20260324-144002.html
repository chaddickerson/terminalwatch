Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 09:58 AM ET — 20 reports across 4 days

Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 09:57 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

Most recent reports from Tuesday morning (Mar 24, 7:47–7:52 AM) show exceptionally fast processing at DEN's west gate checkpoint, with waits of just 2 minutes. Travelers consistently describe the experience as "quick and pain free," with one reporter noting DEN stands out as uniquely efficient compared to other major airports.

Data from Monday (Mar 23) confirms this pattern holds throughout the day. Morning waits ranged from 3–7 minutes (Mar 23, 5:10 AM–7:04 AM), while afternoon and evening processing stayed between 5–20 minutes (Mar 23, 3:30 PM–10:22 PM). One traveler reported 15 minutes on an early Sunday morning (Mar 22, 9:47 AM). PreCheck or dedicated express lanes are not specifically mentioned in these reports, but DEN's newly expanded security checkpoint layout (operational for the past 2 years) appears to support rapid throughput.

The consistency across multiple days and times suggests DEN operates more efficiently than many peer airports. Even during peak travel periods, 20–30 minutes represents the upper range reported (Sun Mar 22, 2:16 PM). Airport staff actively manage queues to keep TSA screening stations fully staffed.

Recommendation: Arrive 30 minutes before your flight departure for domestic travel. DEN's track record does not require the 2-hour buffer typical at congested hubs. If traveling during early morning peak (5–8 AM), 45 minutes is safer, but plan for meaningful buffer time for check-in, not security.
