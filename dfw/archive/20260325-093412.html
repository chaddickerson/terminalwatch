TSA PreCheck continues to move quickly at DFW. A traveler reported 5 minutes in the PreCheck line (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 2:00 PM), and another experienced less than 5 minutes in PreCheck during the afternoon (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:35 PM). One passenger reported TSA PreCheck was 25 minutes early morning (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:39 PM).

Regular TSA lines at DFW have been notably short in recent reports. General screening took less than 15 minutes during the afternoon (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:35 PM), and another traveler cleared security in less than 3 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:14 PM). A TSA PreCheck member at Terminal A reported no line on a Saturday morning (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:11 PM). One flyer reported clearing regular TSA in 12 minutes on Wednesday, March 18 (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:40 PM). Another traveler noted security moved so quickly that the person cleared TSA before the drop-off party left the airport (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 9:04 PM).

One report described unusually heavy congestion: a traveler flying into DFW early morning from Philadelphia encountered a TSA line in the thousands, weaving all over the airport, though this was for an arriving flight and not a DFW departure (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:39 PM). Another passenger from LaGuardia to DFW reported waiting 1 hour and 45 minutes in security (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 10:44 AM).

Recommendation: Based on recent reports, DFW security lines have been moving quickly. TSA PreCheck holders should plan for minimal wait—often under 10 minutes. Regular screening lines are also short, typically under 15 minutes, though arriving 1.5 to 2 hours early for domestic flights remains standard practice. Terminal A appears consistent with airport-wide conditions. However, given one report of unusually heavy delays, building in extra time on weekend mornings or peak travel days remains prudent.
