TSA PreCheck at Terminal A was moving exceptionally fast, with one traveler reporting no wait on Saturday morning despite arriving 2.5 hours early (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:11 PM).

General security screening at DFW has been consistently quick across recent reports. Regular TSA lines took less than 15 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:35 PM), with another traveler clearing security in 12 minutes on Wednesday, March 18 and facing 0 minutes on the return flight (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:40 PM). One traveler reported clearing DFW in less than 3 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:14 PM), and another experienced a line with just one person ahead (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:17 AM).

TSA PreCheck lines have been notably faster. PreCheck was approximately 5 minutes on Sunday (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 2:00 PM) and less than 5 minutes on Tuesday (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:35 PM). At 3 a.m., PreCheck was 25 minutes, significantly shorter than regular lines (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:39 PM).

One report described an unusually fast experience: a traveler cleared TSA before the person dropping him off had even exited the airport on Saturday (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 9:04 PM).

One outlier report noted a wait of 1 hour 45 minutes for parents arriving at DFW after flying from LaGuardia on Sunday (Bluesky - Sun Mar 22, 10:44 AM).

Recommendation: Recent data suggests DFW security is moving efficiently. Travelers with TSA PreCheck should plan to arrive 1.5–2 hours early for domestic flights; regular screening passengers should plan for 2–2.5 hours. However, given the significant variation in wait times and the outlier report of 1 hour 45 minutes, arriving earlier rather than later remains the safest approach, especially during peak travel periods or early morning flights.
