TSA PreCheck lines at IAD have been moving exceptionally fast today. A traveler reported 10 minutes through TSA Pre (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:28 AM), and another waited just 11 minutes overall at security (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:56 AM). A separate traveler noted that the longest wait today was actually at the United baggage drop rather than TSA (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:11 AM).

Yesterday, PreCheck was similarly efficient, with one traveler clearing security in just 4 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 3:53 PM). Earlier Wednesday morning, PreCheck had short lines and was operating smoothly, though regular security lines were reported to be longer at that time (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:14 AM). Another traveler experienced virtually no wait—just 30 seconds—during a quiet period (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:01 AM).

Earlier in the week, security moved smoothly with no meaningful waits reported. One traveler found zero wait time at security (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:35 PM), and another described the morning experience as "literally the easiest morning flight I've ever had" with no lines and friendly agents (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 8:13 AM). A midday traveler on Monday also reported no lines at TSA around 12:45 PM (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 1:32 PM).

Recommendation: Based on recent reports, plan to arrive 30–45 minutes before your domestic flight if using TSA PreCheck, as waits have consistently been under 10 minutes. If you're in a regular security line, arrive at least 1–1.5 hours early, though even standard lines appear to have been moving reasonably well this week. PreCheck is clearly the faster option at IAD right now.
