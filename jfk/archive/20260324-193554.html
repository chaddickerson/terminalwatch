TSA Watch: JFK

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 03:22 PM ET — 14 reports across 1 days

Mar 24: 3:21 PM|2:34 PM

Terminal 5: Wait times have been significantly elevated today. A traveler reported that PreCheck took 1 hour and 38 minutes early this morning (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:10 PM). Later in the day, general security lines were around 42 minutes, compared to a typical 21 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:05 PM).

Terminal 4: A traveler waited 51 minutes in the general TSA security line (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:34 PM). However, CLEAR combined with PreCheck performed much better—one traveler got through in 20 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 2:24 PM).

Terminal 8: PreCheck moved quickly, with a traveler reporting under 4 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 2:47 PM).

Terminal 7: Security lines were consistently elevated throughout the afternoon. Wait times held at 29 minutes, which is 15–16 minutes longer than typical (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:03 PM).

One traveler reported that general TSA lines at JFK were not particularly bad, completing security in just under an hour (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:25 PM). However, another traveler noted that the TSA app was not working properly and reported times were significantly underestimated—an initial estimate of 15–30 minutes turned out to be over 2 hours (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 10:09 AM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 2–2.5 hours early for domestic flights, particularly at Terminal 5, where PreCheck experienced extended delays this morning. Terminal 4 and Terminal 8 show a significant advantage for PreCheck and CLEAR users—if you have either, expect substantially shorter waits. Terminal 7 waits remain elevated above normal but more manageable. Regular security lines are consistently slower; factor in extra time if you don't have PreCheck or CLEAR.
