At Terminal 8, a traveler with TSA PreCheck and CLEAR reported a wait of approximately 25 minutes on Tuesday evening, though the regular line appeared significantly longer (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:29 PM).

Terminal 4 saw faster PreCheck processing on Tuesday evening, with a wait of less than 15 minutes, though the regular security line was reported as "super long" (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:05 PM).

Terminal 5 had only 3 out of 16 TSA security bays open on Tuesday evening, resulting in long lines, though they were not extending outside the checkpoint area (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:27 PM).

One traveler reported waiting 2 hours at JFK on Tuesday evening with over 300 people in line and only 2 TSA agents staffing the checkpoint, with agents nearing the end of their shifts (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:55 PM).

A Delta traveler noted on Tuesday afternoon that the TSA PreCheck line extended across roughly half the terminal (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:38 PM).

General airport-wide data from Tuesday afternoon indicated a 13-minute wait time, with quietest periods around 8 minutes and busiest periods reaching 20 minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 1:09 PM).

Recommendation: Arrive at least 2–3 hours early if you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, as even these expedited lines have been moving slowly (15–25 minutes reported). If you have standard security, plan for 3–4 hours early arrival given the significant gaps between PreCheck and regular line wait times observed across terminals. Terminal 4 and Terminal 8 appear to be slightly more efficient than Terminal 5 for PreCheck holders based on recent reports.
