At Terminal 4, TSA PreCheck wait times have varied significantly. Early this morning, PreCheck was approximately 30 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:30 AM), though earlier yesterday evening it took less than 15 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:05 PM). Regular security lines at Terminal 4 have been significantly longer, with reports of kiosk congestion and lines extending out the door, though they were moving steadily (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:30 AM). Delta representatives indicated most passengers are arriving 3–4 hours early due to anticipated delays (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:30 AM).

At Terminal 8, a traveler with both TSA PreCheck and CLEAR reported the PreCheck line took approximately 25 minutes to complete, though the regular lines appeared significantly more congested (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:29 PM).

At Terminal 5, only 3 of 16 TSA security bays were open, resulting in long lines, though they were not extending out the door (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:27 PM).

For unspecified terminals, a traveler with PreCheck and Touchless ID reported only a 5-minute TSA wait this morning, though bag tag and drop procedures were chaotic (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:30 AM). Across all JFK terminals, average wait times today are significantly elevated at 83 minutes compared to a typical 11 minutes, a difference of +72 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:58 AM). Yesterday evening, one traveler reported waiting 2 hours with over 300 people in line and only 2 TSA agents present (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:55 PM). The TSA PreCheck line for Delta was also reported as extremely crowded, extending roughly halfway through the terminal (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:38 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 3–4 hours early for flights today, particularly at Terminal 4. If you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, you may see a modest advantage—expect 15–30 minutes in those lines—but all security lanes are experiencing elevated volumes. Regular security lines are substantially longer and moving slowly, so PreCheck or CLEAR membership is especially valuable today.
