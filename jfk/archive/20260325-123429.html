Terminal 7: TSA PreCheck was moving exceptionally fast at 1 minute wait time today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:31 AM), while regular security lines were experiencing 20 minutes of wait time (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:31 AM).

General reports across JFK: Today at 6:59 AM, average wait times across all security lines at JFK were 63 minutes, significantly above the typical 16 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:59 AM). Earlier today at 4:58 AM, wait times had peaked at 83 minutes on average, compared to a typical 11 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:58 AM). One traveler reported waiting 2 or more hours in the TSA line at 5:00 AM and described conditions as "total chaos" (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:03 AM). Another traveler's mother arrived at 2:30 AM and waited 1.5 hours in the regular line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:38 AM). However, one traveler who arrived over 3 hours early made it through security in under 20 minutes today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:32 AM), and another passenger with CLEAR and TSA PreCheck cleared security by 6:25 AM after arriving at the airport around 6:09 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:27 AM). A general report today noted TSA wait times at approximately 30 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:25 AM). Yesterday afternoon, the TSA PreCheck line at Delta in one terminal grew so large it reportedly took up half the terminal (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:38 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive at JFK at least 3–4 hours early for domestic flights today. If you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, you can expect substantially shorter waits — potentially under 5–20 minutes — making it possible to arrive closer to 2.5 hours early. Regular security lines are experiencing 20–83 minute delays, so extra buffer time is essential for standard screening.
