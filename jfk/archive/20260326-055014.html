At Terminal 5, TSA PreCheck was moving significantly faster than regular lines, with PreCheck taking approximately 12 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:55 PM).

At Terminal 8, a traveler reported waiting over 1 hour in the regular security line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:48 PM). The same report noted that when ICE personnel arrived, approximately 80 people were processed in under 15 minutes.

One traveler reported that both PreCheck and CLEAR lines were closed at JFK, with the regular line taking approximately 45 minutes from arrival at the TSA checkpoint through packing the laptop (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:01 PM).

On a Monday flight, a traveler experienced 45 minutes in the TSA PreCheck line and 2 hours 30 minutes in the regular security line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:34 PM). A return flight from Boston on Tuesday showed much shorter waits at 5 minutes.

Recommendation: Arrive at least 2.5 hours before departure if you're in the regular line, as recent reports show waits approaching or exceeding 2 hours. If you have TSA PreCheck, plan for 12–45 minutes, though note that PreCheck lines have occasionally closed. CLEAR availability varies—check its status before arriving. Terminal 5 appears to be moving faster for PreCheck holders than other terminals.
