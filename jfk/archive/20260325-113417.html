Today at 6:09 AM, a traveler with CLEAR and PreCheck cleared TSA security in just 16 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 07:27 AM). However, another report from 5:00 AM today described 2+ hour wait times and "total chaos" in the regular security lines (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 07:03 AM). At 2:30 AM today, a non-PreCheck traveler waited 1.5 hours in line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 05:38 AM).

Terminal 4: A traveler reported that if you don't arrive when TSA opens, "you're basically kinda fucked" over the past two weeks (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 04:55 AM).

Terminal 8: On Tuesday evening, a PreCheck and CLEAR holder waited approximately 25 minutes in the PreCheck line, though the regular line appeared significantly longer (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 09:29 PM).

Overall wait times across JFK have been significantly elevated. At 4:58 AM today, average wait times across all lines were 83 minutes (typical: 11 minutes) (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 04:58 AM), and by 6:59 AM today they had decreased slightly to 63 minutes (typical: 16 minutes) (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 06:59 AM). On Tuesday evening, one traveler reported waiting 2 hours with only 2 TSA agents working (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 08:55 PM). The PreCheck line at Delta was reported to stretch across half the terminal on Tuesday afternoon (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 04:38 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 3 hours early for domestic flights and 4 hours early for international flights. If you have TSA PreCheck and/or CLEAR, expect 15–30 minute waits, making a 2–3 hour arrival window sufficient. However, regular security lines are experiencing extreme delays of 1.5–2+ hours, so arriving significantly earlier is essential if you do not have PreCheck or CLEAR.
