Terminal 4: PreCheck travelers experienced notably faster processing today. One traveler with PreCheck got through in approximately 30 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:24 PM), while another combining CLEAR and TSA PreCheck cleared security in 20 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 2:24 PM). A regular security line wait was reported at 51 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:34 PM).

Terminal 7: Regular security lines were running 29 minutes, about 15–16 minutes longer than typical (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:03 PM).

Terminal 5: Regular security wait times were reported at 42 minutes, approximately 21 minutes above the typical average (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:05 PM). However, one traveler reported significantly longer delays, with the TSA app showing an initial estimate of 15–30 minutes that turned into over 2 hours (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 10:09 AM).

Terminal 8: One PreCheck traveler cleared security in under 4 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 2:47 PM).

A general report noted that overall JFK wait times ranged from approximately 20–90 minutes across terminals, and one traveler reported clearing TSA in just under 1 hour (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:25 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 2–2.5 hours early for domestic flights if using a regular security line, particularly at Terminal 5, which has shown extended waits today. If you have TSA PreCheck, you can safely plan for 45 minutes to 1 hour before departure; CLEAR combined with PreCheck offers the fastest option at 20–30 minutes. Terminal 8 PreCheck lines appear to be moving exceptionally fast. Note that the TSA app may underestimate wait times, so consider adding buffer time beyond app estimates.
