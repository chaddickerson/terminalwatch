At Terminal 8, TSA PreCheck moved relatively quickly today, with one traveler completing security in approximately 25 minutes using both PreCheck and CLEAR (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 09:29 PM). However, regular lines appeared significantly backed up at the time of that report.

Terminal 4 saw less than 15 minutes for TSA PreCheck today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 07:05 PM), though regular lines were described as "super long," suggesting a substantial gap between PreCheck and standard screening wait times.

Terminal 5 had only 3 out of 16 TSA screening bays open today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 07:27 PM), resulting in long lines, though they were not extending outside the terminal.

One report mentioned that the TSA PreCheck line for Delta at JFK appeared to span roughly half the terminal today, indicating significant congestion even in expedited screening (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 04:38 PM).

A traveler reported waiting approximately 2 hours at JFK today with over 300 people in line and only 2 TSA agents available, with staff shifts ending (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 08:55 PM).

Earlier today, TSA wait times were predicted at 13 minutes across JFK, with busiest times reaching 20 minutes and quietest times around 8 minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 01:09 PM).

Recommendation: If you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, plan to arrive 1.5 to 2 hours before your flight; lines have been moving under 25 minutes for eligible travelers. Without PreCheck, arrive significantly earlier—3 to 4 hours minimum—as regular security lines have shown substantial delays and inconsistent staffing levels across terminals today. Terminal 4 appears to be processing PreCheck faster than other terminals, if you have a choice of departure points.
