At Terminal 5, a traveler who cleared security at 11:40 AM today reported very few conveyor belts open and slow-moving lines, though they noted this was an improvement over the chaos earlier in the morning (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:13 PM). Earlier today at 10:20 AM, another traveler reported no lines at all (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:26 AM).

At Terminal 4, TSA PreCheck showed 10–20 minute wait times at 10:50 AM today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:53 AM). A report from yesterday at 10:30 AM noted no line whatsoever in the PreCheck line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:00 PM).

At Terminal 8, the general security line was very long—wrapped around the entry hall—while the TSA PreCheck line took approximately 10 minutes to clear this morning (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:15 AM).

Across JFK overall, wait times today have been significantly elevated. The average wait time across all lines reached 64 minutes this morning, compared to a typical average of 13 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:01 AM). An early morning traveler at 5:00 AM today reported experiencing a 2+ hour line with total chaos (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 07:03 AM). Live wait time reporting has been suspended at JFK, LGA, and EWR as of 11:23 AM today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:23 AM). A separate report noted 20-minute waits at JFK and EWR this afternoon (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:17 PM).

Recommendation: TSA PreCheck and regular lines are experiencing vastly different conditions today. If you have PreCheck, plan for 10–20 minutes1–2 hours for security screening. Terminal 4 and Terminal 5 PreCheck lines appear faster than Terminal 8's general security. Arrive as early as possible; morning rush (5:00–11:00 AM) has seen the worst congestion.
