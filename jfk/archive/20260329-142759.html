Airport Update: JFK

Terminal 8: TSA PreCheck showed dramatic improvement by mid-morning. At 75 minutes in the priority line (Bluesky - Sun Mar 29, 9:34 AM), TSA PreCheck was moving "a lot faster" than regular and priority lines. Earlier today, 105 minutes was reported for PreCheck (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 10:14 AM), though one traveler with PreCheck experienced 1 hour 15 minutes starting around 6 AM (Bluesky - Sun Mar 29, 7:29 AM).

Terminal 4: Wait times varied significantly. Reports showed 105 minutes average (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 9:42 AM), though one traveler with TSA PreCheck waited only 45 minutes (Bluesky - Sun Mar 29, 6:49 AM). Notably, CLEAR performed poorly—one user reported CLEAR at 70 minutes versus TSA PreCheck at 10 minutes (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 7:37 AM). However, at 8:45 AM, another traveler found no line, with under 10 minutes (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 8:41 AM).

Terminal 5: Conditions were severe. 105 minutes was the standard report (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 9:31 AM), but one traveler warned these numbers are misleading—add at least an hour for a separate pre-security line just to access the terminal (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 9:02 AM). One PreCheck traveler waited 43 minutes plus 17 minutes for bag check (Bluesky - Sun Mar 29, 8:12 AM).

Terminal 1: One traveler reported security taking only 2 minutes (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 9:36 AM).

Overall, 105 minutes above typical was reported across all terminals (Twitter - Sun Mar 29, 8:54 AM).

Recommendation: Arrive 3.5–4 hours early without checked luggage, or 5 hours with a bag (Bluesky - Sun Mar 29, 10:09 AM). Terminal 5 requires additional buffer time due to pre-security crowding. TSA PreCheck offers faster processing at most terminals, though Terminal 4 CLEAR users experienced unusual delays today.
