At Terminal 8, the general security line was very long early this morning, wrapping around the entry hall, while the TSA PreCheck line moved much faster at approximately 10 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:15 AM).

At Terminal 5, a traveler showed their passport and was checked by TSA at 11:40 AM today after arriving at 11:15 AM, noting very few conveyor belts were open and service was at a snail's pace compared to normal (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:13 PM).

At Terminal 4, a TSA PreCheck passenger experienced virtually no wait, walking directly through the line at 10:30 AM for a 1:10 PM flight (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:00 PM).

Across JFK generally, wait times today have been significantly elevated. The airport reported an average wait of 64 minutes across all security lines—51 minutes longer than the typical 13 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:01 AM). One traveler reported waiting approximately 45 minutes to get through security today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:16 PM). However, other travelers reported much shorter experiences: one passenger cleared TSA in less than 10 minutes each way on a round trip (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:07 PM), and another got through TSA PreCheck in just 5 minutes after arriving at 11 AM for a 1 PM flight (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:02 PM). Earlier this morning, at least one traveler reported waiting over 2 hours in line at 5 AM (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:03 AM).

Recommendation: Arrive at least 2–2.5 hours before your flight today, as conditions are highly unpredictable across terminals. If you have TSA PreCheck, you may experience significantly shorter waits (potentially 5–10 minutes), but general security lines are backed up substantially—Terminal 8's regular line was particularly congested this morning. Terminal 4 and 5 showed mixed results, so do not rely on a specific terminal being faster.
