Terminal 4 experienced significant congestion today. Wait times in the regular security line reached 83 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:00 PM), well above the typical 23-24 minutes. However, TSA PreCheck travelers fared much better—one traveler reported only 10 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:22 PM), and another with PreCheck encountered no line at all when arriving at 10:30 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:00 PM).

Terminal 5 reported slow movement today. A traveler who cleared TSA at 11:40 AM noted very few conveyer belts were open and the pace was considerably slower than normal (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:13 PM).

Across all terminals at JFK, average wait times climbed throughout the morning. By 2:00 PM today, the airport-wide average had reached 64 minutes, compared to a typical 23 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:58 PM). Earlier in the afternoon at 1:04 PM, the average was 44 minutes versus a typical 18 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:04 PM). Some travelers reported much shorter waits—under 10 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:07 PM) and around 20 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:17 PM)—suggesting variability by time and location.

Early morning security screening was particularly backed up. A traveler reported waiting over 2 hours in line at 5:00 AM today, describing conditions as chaotic (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:03 AM). The previous day, a TSA PreCheck line for Delta was reported to span roughly half the terminal (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:38 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 2 to 2.5 hours early for domestic flights, especially if traveling through Terminal 4 or during peak hours (late morning/early afternoon). If you have TSA PreCheck, expect significantly shorter waits—10 minutes or less in many cases—so you can arrive closer to the standard 1.5 hours early. Avoid the early morning rush if possible, as 5:00 AM screening showed extreme delays. Terminal 4 PreCheck holders have a clear advantage today, while regular security lines there remain substantially longer than normal.
