Airport Update: SFO

Early this morning today, a traveler with TSA PreCheck experienced an unusually busy security line at 3:30 AM, reporting that the standard security checkpoint was "wildly long" (Bluesky - Thu Mar 26, 5:34 AM). However, later today, another traveler reported getting through security in just 5 minutes from curb to gate (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 12:54 AM).

On Wednesday, a traveler with Global Entry cleared security in under 90 seconds (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 2:56 PM). Earlier that day, another traveler reported a 3-minute wait (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:34 AM).

On Tuesday morning, a TSA PreCheck passenger who arrived at 8:30 AM for a 10:30 AM flight was the only person in the PreCheck line and cleared security in 3 minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 5:58 PM). That same day at midday, a traveler noted that SFO uses private security contractors, resulting in "basically no wait" (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 12:30 PM).

On Monday, a traveler cleared security with a 2-minute wait (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 2:42 PM). One observer noted that SFO uses TSA-licensed private security contractors rather than TSA staff, and that "wait times haven't increased significantly" (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 1:56 PM).

Recommendation: SFO security lines are generally fast, with most reports showing waits under 5 minutes. PreCheck and Global Entry holders can expect even faster processing, often under 90 seconds. Arriving 60–90 minutes before domestic departure should be sufficient for most travelers; those flying early morning (before 6 AM) may encounter busier-than-usual conditions, so allow extra time if you have an overnight flight.
