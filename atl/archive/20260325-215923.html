TSA PreCheck wait times today have varied significantly throughout the day. Early afternoon saw extended delays, with 1 hour 36 minutes reported (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:20 PM) and over 1 hour in at least one instance (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:08 PM). However, later in the afternoon, a traveler cleared PreCheck in 40 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 1:41 PM), and another reported 5 minutes the previous day (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:17 PM). Around midday, PreCheck and CLEAR were roughly equivalent at 45 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:26 PM), though another traveler reported 50 minutes through PreCheck at 1 PM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:19 PM). International lines moved faster, with a 10-minute wait reported (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:19 PM).

Regular security lines have also experienced delays today. One traveler reported 40 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:01 PM), and earlier in the day the line wrapped through baggage claim and out the door for a passenger facing a flight 3.5 hours away (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:47 AM). A general report indicated average wait times across all lines were 67 minutes (50 minutes above typical) as of early evening today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:07 PM), though an earlier check at 3:04 PM showed 46 minutes average (30 minutes above typical) (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 3:04 PM).

ATL airport has experienced severe TSA staffing shortages due to a partial shutdown, with call-outs at approximately 37% and over 400 national quits reported (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:07 PM). One traveler arrived 4 hours early today and had about 3 hours to spare after clearing security (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 4:21 PM). Another passenger experienced an overnight ordeal, waiting from approximately 5:00 PM Sunday until 1:30–1:40 AM to get through TSA, resulting in missed flights and work call-outs (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 3:43 PM). One traveler who arrived at 12:23 PM for a 5:20 PM flight yesterday reported 30 minutes through TSA (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:38 PM). Staff have been described as helpful and efficient despite the strain (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:19 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 4+ hours early for all flights from ATL (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:07 PM). PreCheck and regular lines are both experiencing significant delays—PreCheck has ranged from 40 minutes to over 1.5 hours today, so do not assume an expedited line will get you through quickly. Lines appear slightly shorter in late afternoon compared to midday peak hours. Arrive substantially earlier than usual to account for variable wait times.
