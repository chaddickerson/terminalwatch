PreCheck wait times today at ATL have been highly variable throughout the day. This evening, one traveler waited 1 hour to get through PreCheck (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:38 PM), while another reported 1 hour and 36 minutes in the PreCheck line (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:20 PM). Earlier in the afternoon, wait times were somewhat better: one PreCheck traveler got through in 40 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 1:41 PM), and another reported approximately 45 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 10:23 AM). A traveler in a regular security line waited 45 minutes today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:58 PM).

Overall wait times across all security lines at ATL today have been significantly elevated. As of mid-afternoon, the average wait was 67 minutes compared to a typical 17 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:07 PM), and earlier in the evening the average was reported at 50 minutes versus a typical 7 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:09 PM). One traveler reported experiencing a 2-hour queue at a single screener station during peak afternoon hours, with some passengers facing 3–4 hour waits (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:18 PM). This morning, conditions were severe: one traveler's spouse reported the TSA line wrapping through baggage claim and out the door with a 3.5-hour window before departure (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:47 AM).

One traveler noted that this afternoon there were no lines and no crowds at ATL (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:34 PM), though this appears to be an outlier compared to other reports from the same timeframe. A report from the previous Thursday afternoon indicated no wait at PreCheck (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:16 PM).

Plan to arrive 3–4 hours early for flights today at ATL. PreCheck lines have ranged from 40 minutes to over 1.5 hours, while regular security lines have shown similar variability. Current conditions suggest PreCheck offers modest advantages over regular lines, though both have experienced significant delays today. Allow extra buffer time, especially during peak hours (morning and late afternoon), and consider using MARTA or ground transportation if ride-sharing services are experiencing delays.
