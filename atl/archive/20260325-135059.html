The international terminal at ATL had zero minutes wait time earlier today, though a traveler noted it had been approximately 1 hour earlier in the morning (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:22 AM).

PreCheck lines today have been significantly longer than normal. One traveler waited over 2 hours in the PreCheck line this morning, though they were moved to the front due to traveling with a double stroller (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:29 AM). Another traveler reported 1 hour wait time in PreCheck at 7 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:07 AM), and a traveler who arrived at 5 AM today experienced 1 hour in PreCheck (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:37 AM). Earlier on Tuesday, a traveler cleared PreCheck in just over 1 hour at 7:49 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM).

General airport security today has seen highly variable wait times depending on time of day and flight schedule. The TSA reported average wait times across all lines at 74 minutes, compared to a typical 7 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:58 AM). One traveler made it through in 30 minutes today with lines moving smoothly (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:00 AM), while another reported 34 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:53 AM). However, one spouse reported the TSA line wrapping through baggage claim and out the door for a 3.5-hour-away departure (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:47 AM). A traveler who went through at 5:30 AM cleared security by 6:30 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:49 AM).

Multiple reports indicate delays are concentrated in early morning flights. A frequent ATL flyer noted afternoon and evening flights typically have under 1 hour waits (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:24 AM). Another traveler who flew yesterday at 4 PM showed up at 11:40 AM and got through TSA in 23 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:38 AM). A resident who regularly takes afternoon flights reported consistently spending no more than 30 minutes in TSA lines (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:09 AM). On Tuesday, a traveler made it from the MARTA train through security in only 7.5 minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:37 PM).

Earlier reports from Monday showed severe delays. One traveler waited 2+ hours on Monday with lines starting outside the building (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:04 AM). A report from the previous week mentioned a 2-hour wait (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:51 AM). Last Wednesday, a traveler experienced a 2-hour line just to reach the TSA line, with PreCheck suspended (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 3–4 hours early if you have an early morning flight today, as PreCheck and regular security lines are experiencing significant delays. If possible, book an afternoon or evening flight—those departures are moving through security in under 30 minutes on average. PreCheck lines are running about 1 hour this morning, compared to regular lines experiencing 1–2+ hour waits during peak morning hours. The international terminal remains your fastest option if available for your destination.
