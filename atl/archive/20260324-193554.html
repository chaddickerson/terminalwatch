TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 03:21 PM ET — 12 reports across 1 days

Mar 24: 3:21 PM|2:34 PM

TSA PreCheck at the South Terminal showed no wait at 1:30 p.m. today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:09 PM). However, early-morning PreCheck experiences were mixed: one traveler waited just over an hour at 7:49 a.m. (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM), while another noted PreCheck was moving "somewhat quickly" at 7:33 a.m. (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:33 AM). By 7:10 a.m., the PreCheck line had begun to even out, with the queue starting at baggage carousel 1 rather than extending outside (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM). At 7:23 a.m., the PreCheck starting area was noted as "very full" (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:23 AM). One traveler reported that a friend in general security cleared screening before the PreCheck line had even entered its gates at 7:26 a.m. (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:26 AM).

Standard TSA lines are averaging 35–40 minutes today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:16 PM). One traveler reported a complete screening experience (arrival through bag drop and security) of approximately 2 hours at an unspecified time today (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM).

Based on today's reports, PreCheck is currently the faster option, with minimal waits in the afternoon and moderate waits early morning. Standard lines are running 35–40 minutes. If you have PreCheck, plan to arrive 90 minutes to 2 hours before departure; without PreCheck, plan for at least 2–2.5 hours. Afternoon travel appears to be lighter than morning.
