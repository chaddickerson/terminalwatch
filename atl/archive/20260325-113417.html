Today at ATL, general security screening showed mixed results depending on arrival time. A traveler who arrived at 5:55 AM reported that the general security check at the International Concourse took only 10 minutes, though PreCheck did not open until 7:00 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 07:04 AM). Another traveler who arrived at 5:15 AM cleared security in 1 hour 5 minutes and reached their gate by 6:35 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 06:49 AM). A traveler in the north terminal domestic security checkpoint got in line around 2:15 AM and made it through in 45 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 03:17 AM). However, wait times increased significantly during morning rush hours. One traveler reported 90 minutes through TSA PreCheck (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 06:43 AM), while another spent 85 minutes in PreCheck between 4:25–5:50 AM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 05:52 AM). A third traveler who arrived at 3:45 AM reported 1.5 hours overall with heavy lines already building (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 05:50 AM). One traveler reported waiting 45 minutes from the check-in area to the train (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 07:01 AM).

Reports from earlier this week indicate significantly longer waits during peak hours. On Tuesday morning, a traveler cleared TSA PreCheck in just over 1 hour at 7:49 AM, with at least ten agents checking IDs and Delta personnel assisting in the bin area (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 07:58 AM). However, another traveler noted that at the same time, a friend in the general security line cleared before the PreCheck line did (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 07:28 AM). An earlier report from last Wednesday noted a traveler spent 2.5 hours total when arriving at 7:00 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 04:10 PM). On Monday, one traveler experienced 2+ hours to get through security with lines extending outside the building (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 07:04 AM).

For the best experience, arrive very early—ideally 3–4 hours before departure during this period. Early morning departures before 5:00–5:30 AM appear to move faster (45 minutes to 1 hour), but mid-morning flights (6:00–8:00 AM) have faced significantly longer delays, with PreCheck lines reaching 90 minutes or more. General security lines and PreCheck have shown variable performance; arriving well before the morning rush provides the most predictable clearance times.
