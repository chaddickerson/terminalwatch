TSA PreCheck wait times at ATL today have been highly variable. The most recent report from mid-afternoon indicates a wait of 1 hour and 36 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:20 PM). However, earlier in the afternoon, PreCheck times ranged significantly: 75 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:08 PM), over 1 hour (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:17 PM), at least 2 hours (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:55 PM), 40 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 1:41 PM), and 45 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:26 PM). At midday, a 50-minute wait was reported for PreCheck with international lines at just 10 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:19 PM). Earlier today, 40 minutes for PreCheck was reported around midmorning (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 1:41 PM), and one traveler reported getting through in about 1 hour despite long lines (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 9:54 AM). One traveler reported an unusually quick 5-minute wait through PreCheck yesterday (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:17 PM).

CLEAR and standard security lines showed similar congestion. A report from mid-afternoon noted that CLEAR and PreCheck were roughly equal at 45 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:26 PM).

Overall wait times across ATL today have been substantial. A travel advisory reported delays of 2–5+ hours system-wide (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 3:53 PM). One traveler described arriving 4 hours early and still having only about three hours to spare after completing security (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 4:21 PM). Another account from early morning indicated the TSA line wrapped through baggage claim and out the airport door (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:47 AM). A report from Sunday evening described a traveler waiting from 5:00 PM until 1:30–1:40 AM to clear security (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 3:43 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 3–4 hours early for your flight at ATL. PreCheck and standard security have experienced similar delays today, so PreCheck provides no significant advantage under current conditions. If possible, aim for off-peak times and consider arriving even earlier if you have an early morning flight, as early morning reports indicated extremely long queues.
