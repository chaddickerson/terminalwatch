TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 01:59 PM ET — 18 reports across 2 days

Mar 24: 1:59 PM|1:34 PM|12:37 PM|11:40 AM|11:17 AM|10:40 AM|9:57 AM|9:24 AM

As of Tuesday afternoon (Tue Mar 24, 1:16 PM ET), standard TSA security lines at Atlanta are averaging 35–40 minutes—significantly better than the previous day. This marks a sharp improvement from Monday, when PreCheck lines alone hit 2 hours 45 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 5:37 PM ET). Early reports from Tuesday morning show continued improvement: one traveler cleared PreCheck in just over an hour (Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM ET), while another made it through in 5 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 12:39 PM ET).

PreCheck passengers have seen the most dramatic gains. Reports from the noon hour show 15 minutes in the priority line (Tue Mar 24, 1:04 PM ET), a major shift from the 2+ hour waits on Monday. Earlier Tuesday morning, the PreCheck queue extended into the baggage claim area (Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM ET), but lines stabilized and began shortening by mid-morning (Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM ET).

Arrive 2 hours before your flight if you're flying Tuesday or Wednesday. While current wait times are well below the 4-hour advisory Atlanta was recommending earlier in the week, early morning and evening peaks may still develop. PreCheck remains your fastest option at 15–40 minutes, though standard lines are now moving efficiently as well.
