PreCheck lines at ATL experienced severe congestion early this morning. A traveler who started in the PreCheck line at 4:45 AM today did not clear security until 6:15 AM, reporting that PreCheck wait times had reached a minimum of 1.5 hours and were likely growing to 2+ hours with lines extending out the door, with no CLEAR or Touchless ID option available at the Domestic Security Checkpoint (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:24 AM). In contrast, another traveler on the international side reported getting through security much faster, entering the TSA line at 4:40 AM today and clearing by 5:20 AM (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:53 AM).

On Wednesday, PreCheck wait times at ATL remained highly variable. One traveler reported waiting 1 hour in the PreCheck line in the evening (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:38 PM), while others experienced significantly shorter waits of 45 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:58 PM) and 45 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:58 PM). Another traveler reported a 1 hour 36 minute wait through PreCheck (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:20 PM). A midday traveler on Wednesday got through PreCheck in 40 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 1:41 PM), and another reported about 45 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 10:23 AM). One traveler noted getting through in about an hour on Wednesday morning (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 9:54 AM).

One traveler reported getting through both ATL and Newark security in under 40 minutes this week for afternoon flights (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:02 PM). A separate report noted that TSA lines at ATL on Wednesday wrapped through baggage claim and extended out the door, with one person waiting in line outside the airport for a flight departing in 3.5 hours (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:47 AM).

Recommendation: Conditions are highly unpredictable at ATL right now. If flying today or in the coming days with a PreCheck membership, plan to arrive at least 2–2.5 hours before departure, as wait times have ranged from 45 minutes to over 1.5 hours—and early morning departures have been particularly congested. Regular security line travelers should add even more time. Note that CLEAR and Touchless ID options have been reported as unavailable or limited at the Domestic Security Checkpoint during peak periods, so do not rely on those for faster processing.
