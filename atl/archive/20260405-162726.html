Today at ATL's Terminal Domestic – Main checkpoint, wait times reached 53 minutes, which is 30 minutes longer than typical (Twitter - Sun Apr 05, 4:05 AM). This represents a significant increase compared to reports from yesterday morning, when most checkpoints were running much faster.

Yesterday morning, conditions improved substantially as the day progressed. The Main, North, and Lower North checkpoints were all under 10 minutes, while the South PreCheck-only line had no wait (Twitter - Sat Apr 04, 7:54 AM). Earlier yesterday, 40-minute waits were reported, but lines shortened considerably by mid-morning (Bluesky - Sat Apr 04, 7:54 AM). Travelers with PreCheck experienced notably faster service, with 10-minute waits reported yesterday (Twitter - Sat Apr 04, 9:22 AM).

Arrive at least 1 hour early today for standard screening, given current 53-minute delays at the main terminal. PreCheck holders should plan an extra 30–45 minutes to be safe, as lines have been variable. Consider flying during early morning or late-night hours if flexible—night flights averaged only 4-minute waits versus 25 minutes during morning hours (Twitter - Sat Apr 04, 5:48 AM).
