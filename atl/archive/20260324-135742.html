TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET — 14 reports across 2 days

Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

ATL is experiencing severe congestion. TSA quoted 3 hours for both PreCheck and general screening (Tue Mar 24, 5:59 AM), and the airport has disabled wait-time signs because lines are extending into the baggage claim area. Clear is currently closed. Early morning reports show PreCheck lines starting in the loading/unloading zone (Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM), though conditions have been improving slightly as the morning progresses—by 7:10 AM, the external overflow had cleared and queues were beginning at baggage carousel 1 (Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM).

PreCheck performance varies dramatically. One traveler reported just over 1 hour through PreCheck with 10 TSA agents working and Delta staff assisting with bins (Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM), while another noted a general security passenger cleared faster than they did through PreCheck (Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM). Historical data shows 2 hours 45 minutes in the PreCheck line on Monday morning (Mon Mar 23, morning) and 2 hours total from arrival through screening including bag drop on Tuesday early morning (Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM). Monday evening saw lines clear by 9:30 PM after hitting 4–5 hours in the afternoon (Mon Mar 23, afternoon through 9:30 PM).

Arrive at least 4 hours early. This matches ATL's own current recommendation (Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM). Even PreCheck holders should expect significant delays. If possible, consider late evening flights when congestion drops dramatically, or check real-time conditions before heading to the airport.
