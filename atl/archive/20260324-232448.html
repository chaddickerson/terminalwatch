TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 06:27 PM ET — 23 reports across 1 days

Mar 24: 6:27 PM|5:25 PM|4:27 PM|3:35 PM|3:21 PM|2:34 PM

PreCheck wait times today have been highly variable. Early this morning, a traveler made it through just over an hour (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM), while another reported 2 hours total from arrival through screening (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM). However, by mid-afternoon, PreCheck moved significantly faster—5 minutes at 3:30 PM (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:27 PM) and less than 8 minutes at 2 PM (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:45 PM). One traveler reported a notably slower experience, waiting 2 hours in PreCheck at 5 AM today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:17 PM).

Regular security lines showed dramatic swings. Overall wait times fell from over 3 hours yesterday to under 45 minutes today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:05 PM). A few travelers reported exceptional speed—one cleared in less than a minute (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:08 PM) and another in under 2 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:04 PM). However, earlier in the week conditions were far worse, with one traveler reporting 9 hours and missing their flight (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:47 PM) and another describing a line that snaked through baggage claim and took 2+ hours just to reach the TSA checkpoint (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

General reports note that TSA lines have been averaging 4-hour waits this week (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:46 PM), and earlier today lines hit 4–5 hours before clearing by 9:30 PM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 1:24 AM). A TSA agent indicated that improvements today may be due to the day of the week rather than recent ICE deployment, and warned that conditions could worsen if the partial government shutdown continues through the weekend (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:39 PM). Atlanta is officially recommending travelers allow four hours for security (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

Arrival recommendation: Plan to arrive 3–4 hours early for flights today and this week. Morning flights (especially 7 AM departures) have experienced the longest delays; afternoon departures have moved considerably faster. PreCheck has offered some advantage during peak morning hours but remains unpredictable—waits have ranged from 5 minutes to 2+ hours depending on time of day. If possible, choose afternoon departure times, as lines have cleared significantly by early afternoon.
