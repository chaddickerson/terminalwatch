Tuesday saw significant improvement compared to the weekend. A traveler reported waiting 2.5 hours in TSA PreCheck when ICE first arrived Monday, but when returning on a Tuesday noon flight, found the airport "empty, with no lines" (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:03 PM). Another traveler experienced only a 5-minute wait on Tuesday, compared to 4 hours the day before (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:02 PM). A traveler cleared security in just 7.5 minutes on Tuesday afternoon (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:37 PM). On Tuesday morning, TSA PreCheck took just over 1 hour with at least ten agents working ID checks and Delta personnel assisting with bin operations (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM). At the same time Tuesday morning, a general security line traveler cleared faster than a PreCheck traveler who had started at the same moment (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

Over the weekend, conditions were severe. One traveler reported waiting nearly 4 hours in TSA on Sunday and almost missing his connection (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:42 AM). Last Wednesday, a Delta traveler waited 2.5 hours total entering the line at 7:00 AM, with the line extending through the south concourse and around baggage carousels; PreCheck was suspended at that time (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:10 PM). Another traveler spent 2 hours waiting just to reach the TSA line the same Wednesday, with PreCheck unavailable (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

Recommendation: ATL conditions have improved significantly since the weekend, particularly with ICE assistance, but remain unpredictable today. For mid-week flights, arriving 2–2.5 hours early should be sufficient for most travelers. If you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, expect faster processing, though PreCheck lines have been congested during peak morning hours. For weekend or early-morning flights, plan for 3–4 hours as a precaution. Check real-time wait times closer to your departure time, as conditions vary by hour and terminal.
