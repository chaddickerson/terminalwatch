Wait times at Atlanta Hartsfield-Jackson vary significantly by time of day and security line type. PreCheck lines ranged from under 5 minutes to over 2 hours on Tuesday, March 24. Early morning (Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM–7:23 AM) saw PreCheck queues extending into the baggage claim area and loading zones, with one traveler reporting just over 1 hour (Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM). By mid-morning (Tue Mar 24, 11:52 AM), PreCheck moved faster, with less than 5 minutes reported. General security lines were significantly longer: one traveler spent 2 hours total including bag drop (Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM), while another reported 2–4 hour waits earlier in the week (Mon Mar 23, 5:37 PM).

The most dramatic overnight shift occurred between late Monday night and Tuesday morning. Late Monday evening (Tue Mar 24, 1:24 AM), lines hit 4–5 hours earlier in the day but cleared by 9:30 PM. Early Tuesday morning showed congestion returning, with PreCheck lines extending outside the terminal (Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM), though conditions improved by late morning. Reports suggest additional staffing and support from Delta personnel helped expedite screening in the bin and ID-check areas (Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM and 11:52 AM).

Arrive 2–3 hours early for general security, or 1–2 hours early for PreCheck. Morning hours (before 9 AM) consistently show heavier congestion than mid-morning onward. PreCheck offers a meaningful advantage but is still subject to surge delays during peak hours.
