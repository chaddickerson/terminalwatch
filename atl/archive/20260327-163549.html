Airport Update: ATL

ATL security screening today has shown significant variability. Early morning reports indicated sustained delays, with average wait times across all lines reaching 70 minutes around 9:24 AM (Twitter - Fri Mar 27, 9:24 AM). One traveler reported 2.5+ hours starting at 5:30 AM, though they noted that DHS ICE agents and Delta staff managed the process efficiently (Twitter - Fri Mar 27, 9:26 AM). However, TSA PreCheck experienced longer waits—one traveler waited 1.5 hours in the PreCheck line (Bluesky - Fri Mar 27, 8:27 AM).

By midday, conditions improved dramatically. Multiple travelers reported passing through security in 5–12 minutes (Twitter - Fri Mar 27, 11:09 AM, Twitter - Fri Mar 27, 12:06 PM). One traveler noted PreCheck had no line (Twitter - Fri Mar 27, 11:17 AM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 2–2.5 hours early for domestic flights if traveling during early morning peak hours. PreCheck and CLEAR holders may experience significantly faster processing than standard lines, particularly outside peak times. Afternoon travel appears substantially faster.
