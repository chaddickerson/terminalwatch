TSA PreCheck wait times today have been significantly longer than regular security lines. One traveler reported a 1 hour wait through PreCheck at 7 am (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 08:07 AM), while another experienced 90 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 06:43 AM), and a third waited 85 minutes between 4:25 and 5:50 am (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 05:52 AM).

At the International Concourse today, conditions have improved markedly. A traveler reported that general security took only 10 minutes at 5:55 am, though PreCheck had not yet opened at that hour (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 07:04 AM). Another passenger noted the International Concourse TSA line remained very short this morning (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 08:05 AM).

Overall conditions today are exponentially better than they were over the weekend, with short lines reported this morning (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 08:13 AM). One traveler who arrived at 5:15 am today cleared security in 1 hour 5 minutes and reached their gate by 6:35 am (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 06:49 AM). Earlier today, another passenger reported the Concourse TSA took approximately 45 minutes from the checkpoint to the train (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 07:01 AM).

Yesterday (Monday), conditions were significantly worse. Travelers reported 2+ hour waits with lines extending outside the building (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 07:04 AM) and another 2 hour wait on Monday (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 07:51 AM). On Tuesday evening, a traveler reported that delays appeared limited to the weekend or morning flights, with a 7 and 1/2 minute wait from the MARTA train through security on Tuesday afternoon (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 06:37 PM).

Last week's conditions were severe. On Tuesday, a traveler reported just over 1 hour through PreCheck with at least ten agents working (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 07:58 AM). That same morning at 7:26 am, a passenger entering general security cleared faster than a friend who had started PreCheck at the same time (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 07:28 AM). The previous Wednesday, a Delta passenger experienced 2.5 hours total including the queue to reach TSA, starting their line at 7:00 am (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 04:10 PM).

Recommendation: Today's data shows significant improvement, especially at the International Concourse. If flying early morning (before 7 am), plan for 10-45 minutes at general security, though PreCheck may not yet be open. For typical morning departures (7 am and later), plan at least 1 to 1.5 hours for PreCheck and 45 minutes to 1 hour for general security. Conditions have stabilized considerably compared to the weekend and early-week chaos, but PreCheck lines remain notably slower than general security at present. Arriving 2.5 to 3 hours early for domestic flights remains prudent given current staffing levels.
