TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 08:53 PM ET — 29 reports across 2 days

Mar 24: 8:53 PM|7:24 PM|6:27 PM|5:25 PM|4:27 PM|3:35 PM|3:21 PM|2:34 PM

Early today, ATL TSA PreCheck saw significant congestion. The PreCheck line started in the baggage claim loading/unloading zone at 6:41 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM), though it began to ease by mid-morning. A traveler made it through PreCheck in just over an hour at 7:49 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM), with at least ten agents checking ID. However, general security moved faster than PreCheck during the same period—one traveler who started PreCheck at the same time as a companion in general security reported his companion cleared security first (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

By early afternoon, conditions had improved dramatically. A traveler reported getting through TSA in less than a half hour (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:23 PM), and afternoon PreCheck waits had dropped to approximately 10 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:18 PM). One traveler reported a 2-minute wait at 3 PM (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:25 PM). Another traveler at 10:30 AM noted there seemed to be significantly more TSA agents on duty than in previous days (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:07 PM).

Evening conditions continued to show improvement. By around 7 PM, ATL TSA waits had shrunk to 20–40 minutes across checkpoints (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:13 PM)—a sharp drop from Monday's morning peaks of 3–6 hours. One traveler reported the TSA line was nonexistent today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:15 PM). Another observed that the headline-grabbing delays applied mainly to the weekend or morning flights, and got through security in only 7½ minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:37 PM).

A traveler using CLEAR reported cutting their wait time in half compared to the general line during an earlier busy period (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:55 PM).

One traveler reported waiting 2 hours total to clear security in the South Concourse (Delta's area) after arriving at 7:00 AM last Wednesday (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:10 PM). Another account described a 2½-hour experience last Wednesday with extensive line snaking through the baggage claim area and PreCheck suspended (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM). A report from mid-day earlier indicated earlier in the week TSA lines averaged 4-hour waits (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:46 PM), and one historical report mentioned a wait of 2 hours in PreCheck in the past (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:02 PM).

Recommendation: Based on today's data, conditions have improved significantly. If you're flying this afternoon or evening, expect 20–40 minutes in general security or ~10 minutes in PreCheck. However, morning flights remain slower—arrive at least 2–3 hours early if departing in early morning. If you have PreCheck or CLEAR, you'll move substantially faster than the general line, particularly during morning peaks. Consider checking real-time wait updates before heading to the airport, as times appear highly variable depending on flight schedule peaks.
