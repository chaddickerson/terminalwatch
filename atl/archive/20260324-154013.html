TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 11:17 AM ET — 10 reports across 2 days

Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 11:17 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 10:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:57 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

ATL security lines have improved dramatically as of Tuesday morning (Tue Mar 24). PreCheck passengers reported under 5 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 10:40 AM and 10:30 AM), with one traveler clearing the north checkpoint by T gates in zero minutes (Tue Mar 24, 9:45 AM). Standard lines were also moving quickly during this window. However, early morning remained congested: one PreCheck traveler waited just over an hour with at least ten TSA agents working (Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM), while another traveler reported 2 hours total from arrival through screening, including bag drop-off (Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM).

The situation has improved significantly from the previous week. Last Wednesday (Wed Mar 19), travelers faced 2-hour waits just to reach the TSA line, with queues snaking through baggage claim and PreCheck suspended. Monday morning (Mon Mar 23) saw 1-hour waits for standard security and 2 hours 45 minutes for PreCheck (Mon Mar 23, morning). These represent substantial improvements from those recent conditions.

Arrive at ATL 2 hours before domestic departure if you have PreCheck, and 3 hours before departure without it. Morning rush (before 10 AM) remains busier than mid-morning, so if your flight allows, aiming for a 10:30 AM or later departure will likely mean faster processing based on current patterns.
