A traveler waited nearly 2 hours in the TSA line at ATL today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:32 AM).

On Tuesday, PreCheck wait times varied significantly throughout the day. Early morning on Mar 24, the PreCheck line extended into the baggage claim area and started in the loading/unloading zone (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM). By 7:10 a.m., the external line had cleared and the queue began at baggage carousel 1 (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM). A traveler reported making it through PreCheck in just over 1 hour at 7:49 a.m., with at least ten agents checking ID and Delta personnel assisting in the bin area (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM). Notably, a friend who chose general security at the same time entered and exited before the PreCheck traveler reached the gates (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM). Later that evening, a traveler reported the TSA line was nonexistent (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:15 PM), and another who arrived 6 hours early got through in 30 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 11:16 PM). A returning traveler on a noon flight found the airport nearly empty with no lines (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:03 PM), while another reported only 5 minutes wait today compared to 4 hours the day before (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:02 PM). One traveler took just 7.5 minutes from the MARTA train through security (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:37 PM).

Earlier on Mar 24, a traveler reported waiting 2.5 hours through TSA PreCheck the day ICE first arrived, but the situation had improved dramatically by the time he returned on a noon flight (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:03 PM). A traveler who left the previous Wednesday experienced 2.5 hours waiting, with the PreCheck line snaking through the entire baggage claim area and around carousels (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:10 PM). That same traveler noted ATL was recommending four hours for security, and PreCheck had been suspended, with 2 hours spent just to reach the TSA line (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM). Reports indicated lines averaged 4-hour waits that week and as long as 9 hours for some travelers (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:46 PM).

Recommendation: Wait times at ATL have improved dramatically as of Mar 24 evening and Mar 25 early morning, with several reports of minimal or nonexistent lines and others under 30 minutes. However, early morning hours (6–8 a.m.) still showed longer waits, particularly in PreCheck. If you have PreCheck, plan for 45 minutes to 1 hour during peak morning hours; regular security may actually be faster during congested times. For afternoon and evening flights, 30 minutes should be sufficient. Arrive earlier if flying early morning or on peak travel days. Current conditions suggest the airport is recovering, but conditions remain fluid.
