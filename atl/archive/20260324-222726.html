Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 05:26 PM ET — 18 reports across 1 days

Mar 24: 5:25 PM|4:27 PM|3:35 PM|3:21 PM|2:34 PM

A traveler's husband waited 2 hours at PreCheck early this morning (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:17 PM), while another traveler passed through South Terminal TSA PreCheck in under 8 minutes at 2pm today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:45 PM). TSA PreCheck times improved significantly by afternoon, with one traveler reporting just 5 minutes at 3:30pm today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:27 PM) and another experiencing no line at all at 1:30pm in the South Terminal (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:09 PM). Earlier this morning, a PreCheck traveler made it through in just over an hour at 7:49am with at least ten agents checking ID and Delta personnel assisting in the bin area (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM). By 7:10am, the PreCheck line was evening out with the queue starting at baggage carousel 1 after beginning outside in the loading zone around 6:41am (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM).

Another traveler got through security in less than a minute today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:08 PM). Overall wait times at ATL have been around 1 hour by mid-afternoon today, with no reported incidents (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:38 PM). A traveler arriving at 4:22am reported the bag drop area looked busy, though they later completed screening in about 2 hours total from arrival to end of screening, with polite and efficient TSA staff and no ICE presence (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:23 AM). Earlier reporting indicated that while TSA lines hit 4–5 hours during peak morning hours, the checkpoint cleared by 9:30pm (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 1:24 AM).

One traveler from last Wednesday (Mar 19) reported a 2.5-hour total wait departing early from the Delta south concourse, where the TSA line doubled the length of the concourse and snaked through baggage carousels (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:10 PM). Another account from last Wednesday described the TSA line as so long it wrapped through the baggage claim area with a 2-hour wait just to reach the TSA line itself, during which PreCheck was suspended (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM). One traveler who passed through general security on the same morning actually cleared before a PreCheck traveler who had started ahead of them (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

Recommendation: Arrive at least 2–3 hours early for early morning flights today; morning rush (6–8am) experienced the longest delays. TSA PreCheck holders should expect 1+ hours during peak morning times, though PreCheck lines move significantly faster than regular security and improve markedly by afternoon. If your flight departs after 2pm, PreCheck may take only 5–10 minutes. Regular security lines are substantially slower during morning hours, so arrive even earlier if flying standard security in the morning.
