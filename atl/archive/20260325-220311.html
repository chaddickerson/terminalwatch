PreCheck wait times today varied significantly throughout the day. Early afternoon saw longer delays, with PreCheck taking 1 hour and 36 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:20 PM), over an hour (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:17 PM), and 75 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:08 PM). One report noted a 2-hour wait with three PreCheck lines converging into one (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:55 PM). However, mid-afternoon conditions improved, with PreCheck clearing in 50 minutes at 1 PM (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:19 PM) and 40 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 1:41 PM). By late afternoon, conditions had eased further, with a traveler reporting a 40-minute wait in the regular security line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:01 PM).

This morning saw mixed conditions. One traveler reported unusually short wait times, describing it as "the quickest I've ever gone through atl TSA" (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 10:39 AM), while another noted lines were "a lot shorter at ATL today" with wait times around 45 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 10:23 AM). However, early morning saw severe congestion, with one traveler's spouse reporting the TSA line wrapping "all the way through baggage claim and out the door" around 8:47 AM (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:47 AM).

International security had notably shorter wait times, with one report of a 10-minute wait earlier today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:19 PM). One traveler who arrived with 4 hours early today ended up with about 3 hours to spare after clearing security (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 4:21 PM).

General advisories suggest arriving 4+ hours early for security (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:07 PM). Wait times at ATL remain variable due to ongoing TSA staffing challenges. Lines have shown some improvement compared to earlier in the week—one traveler who waited 6+ hours on Sunday reported the wait dropped to 45 minutes the following day (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:49 PM)—but conditions can shift rapidly throughout the day. PreCheck lines have been particularly unpredictable, ranging from 40 minutes to over an hour and a half today. If possible, use international security if traveling internationally, as those lanes have seen notably shorter waits.
