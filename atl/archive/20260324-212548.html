TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 04:27 PM ET — 13 reports across 1 days

Mar 24: 4:27 PM|3:35 PM|3:21 PM|2:34 PM

At the South Terminal, TSA PreCheck has shown dramatic improvement today. A traveler reported virtually no line at 1:30 PM, taking longer to retrieve their ID than to clear security (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:09 PM). Earlier today, PreCheck was much busier: one traveler waited just over an hour at around 7:49 AM, though the checkpoint had at least ten TSA agents working with Delta personnel assisting in the bin area (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM). Starting around 6:41 AM, the PreCheck line extended into the loading/unloading zone, but by 7:10 AM it had eased and effectively started at baggage carousel 1 (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM). One traveler noted that a friend in the general security line cleared before they even entered the PreCheck gates at 7:26 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

General wait times at ATL today have been manageable in afternoon hours. By 3:30 PM, TSA PreCheck was moving exceptionally fast at just 5 minutes, with the reporter noting that morning flights are typically the busier period (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:27 PM). Around 3:38 PM, overall wait times at ATL were reported at approximately 1 hour with no incidents or line-cutting observed (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 3:38 PM).

Earlier today, arrival conditions were challenging. A traveler reported approximately 2 hours total from arrival through screening, including bag drop-off, at around 6:35 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM). At 4:22 AM, bag drop appeared congested, though the TSA line position was unclear at that time (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:23 AM).

For future reference, morning travel to ATL requires significantly more time than afternoon travel. Plan to arrive 3–4 hours early for morning flights, especially if you're in general security; PreCheck travelers may fare somewhat better during peak morning hours but should still budget substantial time. Afternoon departures appear much more manageable—2 hours early arrival should be adequate, particularly with PreCheck or CLEAR.
