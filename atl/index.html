Airport Update: ATL

TSA PreCheck at ATL showed highly variable wait times today. A traveler reported waiting approximately 1 hour 10 minutes total through security this morning, with PreCheck and regular lines reportedly similar or equal in speed (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:52 AM). Another traveler cleared security in about 50 minutes earlier today (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:09 AM). However, other reports from this morning paint a more congested picture: TSA PreCheck lines were experiencing minimum 1.5 hours with growth expected to 2+ hours, with one traveler starting their line in baggage claim at 4:45 AM and clearing security at 6:15 AM; notably, CLEAR and Touchless ID were not open at the Domestic Security Checkpoint (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:47 AM). On the international side, one traveler reported a much faster experience, getting through security in 40 minutes after starting their line at 4:40 AM (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:53 AM). A traveler also noted waiting 20 minutes just to get inside the security area (Bluesky - Thu Mar 26, 6:59 AM).

Yesterday, Wednesday Mar 25, TSA PreCheck experienced substantial delays. One traveler waited 1 hour to clear PreCheck in the evening and arrived at their gate only to encounter a flight delay (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 8:38 PM). Earlier that afternoon, one traveler reported 1 hour 36 minutes through TSA PreCheck (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 3:20 PM). However, some morning travelers yesterday experienced faster times: one made it through PreCheck in 40 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 1:41 PM), and another reported lines were notably shorter with wait times around 45 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 10:23 AM). One traveler reported getting through in about 1 hour thanks to efficient staff despite long lines (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 9:54 AM).

On Monday Mar 23, wait times were severe. One report indicated TSA wait times of 4–6 hours at Hartsfield-Jackson (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 1:39 PM). A client was unable to depart Atlanta due to a 3–4 hour security line and was considering driving back to Texas instead (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 12:19 PM). Another report noted a 3-hour line at the airport (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 12:37 PM). One traveler experienced 2 hours waiting just to reach security, then spent an additional hour in line outside before entering (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 9:11 AM). Notably, the international terminal showed faster PreCheck processing: one traveler cleared in just 8 minutes on Mar 23 afternoon (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 2:01 PM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive at least 3 hours before your flight if departing from ATL's domestic terminals. Today's reports show highly inconsistent wait times ranging from 40 minutes to well over 90 minutes for PreCheck—avoid relying on PreCheck for speed advantage, as today one traveler was told PreCheck lines matched or exceeded regular security lines. If possible, use the international terminal, which has shown significantly faster throughput. Early morning departures (before 5:30 AM) appear to offer a slight advantage. CLEAR and Touchless ID availability is limited or unavailable at the Domestic Security Checkpoint, so do not count on these services to expedite your passage.
