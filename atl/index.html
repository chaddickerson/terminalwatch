TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 07:25 PM ET — 25 reports across 1 days

Mar 24: 7:24 PM|6:27 PM|5:25 PM|4:27 PM|3:35 PM|3:21 PM|2:34 PM

Early today, PreCheck lines at ATL started long, with the queue beginning in the loading/unloading zone at 6:41 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM). By 7:10 AM, the PreCheck line had somewhat eased, with the queue effectively starting at baggage carousel 1 (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM). One traveler reported making it through PreCheck in just over 1 hour at 7:49 AM, noting at least ten TSA agents were checking ID and Delta personnel were filling gaps in the bin area (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM). Another traveler who arrived around 4:22 AM reported the bag drop area looked "hairy" but had not yet confirmed where the TSA line started (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:23 AM). A separate traveler experienced a total of about 2 hours from arrival through screening (including bag drop) earlier today (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM).

By afternoon and evening today, conditions improved significantly. One traveler passed through PreCheck in less than half an hour (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:23 PM). Another reported clearing PreCheck without a long wait, noting spring break appeared to be over and the airport rush was back to normal (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:56 PM). One traveler made it from the MARTA train through security in just 7.5 minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:37 PM). By 7 PM EDT today, ATL TSA waits across checkpoints had shrunk to 20–40 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:13 PM), down sharply from Monday's peaks of 3–6 hours. At 3 PM today, one report cited a TSA wait time of just 2 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 7:24 PM). Late yesterday, by 9:30 PM, the checkpoint was clear after earlier peaks of 4–5 hours (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 1:24 AM).

Reports from earlier in the week provide context: last Wednesday, one traveler experienced a 2.5-hour wait and reported the line snaking around baggage carousels, with PreCheck suspended (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM). Another traveler also on Wednesday waited 2.5 hours total, having started in line at 7:00 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:10 PM). This week, average TSA waits were estimated at around 4 hours (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:46 PM), though one anecdotal report mentioned a traveler stuck in line for 9 hours and missing a flight (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 4:47 PM).

A traveler who used CLEAR at another airport noted that signing up cut her regular security time in half compared to the standard line wait of 40 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 6:55 PM).

Recommendation: Based on today's data, ATL conditions have improved markedly from earlier in the week. For afternoon and evening flights today, standard security waits appear to be 20–40 minutes, making the earlier airport-wide recommendation of 4 hours excessive for those times. For morning flights, plan for 1–2 hours to allow for potential queues, especially in PreCheck lines. If you have PreCheck or CLEAR, expect significantly faster movement—PreCheck cleared in under an hour this morning, and afternoon PreCheck waits dropped to ~10 minutes. However, if staffing issues persist or peak travel times occur (such as weekend mornings), conditions could return to multi-hour waits. Check real-time updates closer to your flight time for the most accurate guidance.
