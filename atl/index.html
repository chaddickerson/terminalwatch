TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 09:14 AM ET — 14 reports across 2 days

ATL is currently experiencing significant TSA delays. Official guidance recommends arriving 4 hours early (Tue Mar 24, 11:28 AM). Early morning reports from Tuesday show PreCheck lines extending into the baggage claim area and starting in the loading/unloading zone (Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM), with TSA quoting 3 hours for both PreCheck and general security (Tue Mar 24, 9:59 AM). Wait time signs have been turned off because lines are too long to track accurately.

Morning conditions improved slightly as the day progressed. By 7:10 AM (Tue Mar 24), the exterior overflow had cleared and the PreCheck queue started at baggage carousel 1. One traveler reported taking just over an hour through PreCheck with approximately ten TSA agents working and Delta personnel assisting in the bin area (Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM). However, another traveler noted that general security moved faster than PreCheck at the same time (Tue Mar 24, 7:26 AM), and earlier in the morning the PreCheck area was described as "very full" (Tue Mar 24, 7:23 AM).

Broader trends show extreme volatility. Reports from the previous day indicate TSA lines hit 4–5 hours in early afternoon but cleared by 9:30 PM (Tue Mar 24, 5:24 AM). A traveler last Wednesday experienced a 2-hour wait just to reach the TSA line, with PreCheck temporarily suspended (Tue Mar 24, 11:28 AM). Most recently, a complete screening including bag drop took about 2 hours (Tue Mar 24, 10:35 AM).

Plan to arrive at least 4 hours before departure. Conditions are unpredictable and dependent on flight schedules, so extra buffer time is essential. PreCheck offers some advantage but is not guaranteed to be significantly faster during peak periods. Avoid peak morning hours (6–8 AM) if possible.
