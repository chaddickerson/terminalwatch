Atlanta TSA wait times show significant variation across the morning of Tuesday, March 24. Early reports from 6:35–7:28 AM indicated 1–2 hour waits in the PreCheck line (Tue Mar 24, 6:35–7:28 AM), with the general security line extending into the baggage claim area. By mid-morning, conditions improved sharply: PreCheck waits dropped to 3–5 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 10:40 AM–11:15 AM), and general security lines became short and fast-moving (Tue Mar 24, 10:33–11:39 AM). This marks a dramatic shift from Monday's experience, when PreCheck alone hit 2 hours 45 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 5:37 AM).

Context from the previous day (Monday, March 23) shows why current conditions stand out. Morning waits stretched to 4–5 hours at the checkpoint (Mon Mar 23, 1:24 AM), though lines cleared by 9:30 PM. Reports also noted extended lines snaking through baggage claim with difficulty locating the queue end (Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM report of prior Wednesday conditions).

If traveling Tuesday afternoon or evening, aim to arrive 2 hours before departure based on mid-morning clarity. For early morning flights (before 7 AM), allow an extra hour as a buffer—early-morning congestion appears to persist before conditions ease by mid-morning.
