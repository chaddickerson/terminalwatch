TSA Watch: ATL

Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 02:50 PM ET — 18 reports across 1 days

Mar 24: 2:34 PM

A traveler waited just over an hour in TSA PreCheck at ATL today (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:58 AM), with at least ten agents checking ID and Delta personnel filling in gaps in the bin area. Earlier today, PreCheck lines started in the baggage carousel area at 7:10 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM), though the queue had initially extended into the loading zone at 6:41 AM (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:41 AM). One traveler reported that general security lines were actually moving faster than PreCheck at the same time this morning (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 7:28 AM).

Standard TSA lines at ATL are averaging 35–40 minutes today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:16 PM), which is less than average. The priority line saw waits of only 15 minutes at noon today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:04 PM). One traveler who expected a 4-hour wait got through in 1.5 hours (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:04 PM), while another made it through in about 1 hour despite long lines (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:23 PM). However, one traveler reported being stuck in a TSA line for nine hours and missing his flight (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 2:17 PM).

Another traveler experienced 2 hours total from arrival through screening, including bag drop-off, with polite and efficient TSA staff and no visible ICE presence (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 6:35 AM).

Recommendation: Plan to arrive 2–3 hours before your domestic flight at ATL today. PreCheck holders should expect 1–1.5 hours35–40 minutes on average, though some travelers have reported faster movement. Conditions have improved significantly from recent days, but the morning commute (6:00–8:00 AM) showed longer queues, so consider arriving even earlier if you're traveling then.
