Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 01:34 PM ET|Mar 24, 2026 12:37 PM ET|Mar 24, 2026 11:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 11:17 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 10:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:57 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

As of Tuesday afternoon (Tue Mar 24, 1:16 PM ET), standard TSA security lines at ATL are averaging 35–40 minutes—below the airport's normal average. TSA PreCheck lines are running significantly faster, with reports of 10–15 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 12:04 PM ET) and even 5 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 12:39 PM ET). This marks a sharp improvement from Monday, when PreCheck waits hit 2 hours 45 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 5:37 PM ET).

Early morning (Tue Mar 24, 6:41–7:10 AM ET) saw longer queues, with PreCheck lines extending into the baggage claim area. However, by mid-morning (Tue Mar 24, 7:10 AM ET), the external overflow had cleared and lines were consolidating at baggage carousel 1. One traveler reported just over 1 hour for PreCheck including bag drop-off (Tue Mar 24, 7:49 AM ET).

Arrive 1.5 to 2 hours early for a domestic flight if you're in the standard line; PreCheck holders can plan for 30–45 minutes. ATL has recovered significantly from prior-day delays, so current conditions should hold unless new disruptions occur.
