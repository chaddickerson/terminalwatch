Last updated: Mar 24, 2026 03:23 PM ET — 17 reports across 2 days

Mar 24: 3:21 PM|2:34 PM

PreCheck with touchless technology has been moving exceptionally fast today, with multiple travelers reporting 2–5 minute waits (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:36 PM, Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:41 PM, Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:29 AM). One traveler noted being the only person in the touchless PreCheck line (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:41 PM). In contrast, the regular security line at MIA was running around 15–20 minutes during the afternoon (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 8:12 PM), though one report from early this morning mentioned 25 minutes in the regular line (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM).

CLEAR performance has been mixed. One traveler with CLEAR waited 5 minutes at MIA yesterday evening (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM), while another reported a 2-hour wait with CLEAR at a different airport (MSY) but then breezed through MIA in 5 minutes when arriving 3 hours early (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 8:12 PM).

One traveler reported snaking lines at the international connecting flight checkpoint at MIA earlier today (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:51 AM), suggesting that specific checkpoint or time of day experienced heavier traffic. Another report from earlier this morning noted 20 minutes for Global Entry clearance at Miami TSA (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 3:27 PM).

Recommendation: If you have PreCheck or CLEAR with touchless technology, plan to arrive 15–20 minutes before your security checkpoint time—today's data shows these programs are moving very efficiently. For regular security lines, plan to arrive 30–40 minutes early, though afternoon waits appear to be running closer to 15–20 minutes. If you're connecting on an international flight, allow extra time as one checkpoint showed noticeably longer lines. Avoid arriving excessively early; several travelers noted they had several hours of idle time in the terminal.
