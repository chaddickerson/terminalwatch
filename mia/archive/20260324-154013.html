As of Tuesday morning, MIA is experiencing minimal wait times. Reports from early today (Tue Mar 24, 5:56 AM – 10:29 AM) consistently show no lines or just a few minutes at TSA checkpoints. One traveler reported 3 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 10:29 AM), while another noted no lines at all (Tue Mar 24, 09:15 AM).

Monday evening (Mon Mar 23) painted a clearer picture of typical wait patterns: PreCheck and CLEAR users experienced under 5 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM – 8:12 PM), while the regular TSA line ranged from 15–25 minutes during the same periods. Earlier that day (Mon Mar 23, 3:27 PM), standard security took around 20 minutes. Morning data from the previous day showed 6 minutes through TSA at 9 AM (reported Tue Mar 24, 08:17 AM for Mon Mar 23 travel).

Arrive 1.5–2 hours early for domestic flights if using the regular line. If you have PreCheck or CLEAR, 45 minutes to 1 hour should be sufficient based on current patterns. Note that congestion at MIA extends beyond TSA—one traveler reported a 30–40 minute wait to enter the Admirals Club (Tue Mar 24, 09:15 AM), so plan buffer time accordingly.
