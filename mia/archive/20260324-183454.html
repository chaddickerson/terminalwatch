TSA wait times at Miami (MIA) have been notably short over the past 24 hours. PreCheck and touchless options are running exceptionally fast, with multiple travelers reporting 2–5 minute waits (Tue Mar 24, 1:36 PM through 10:29 AM ET). Standard TSA lines are longer but still manageable: 15–25 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM and 6:30 PM ET), with one report of 30–40 minute delays entering the Admirals Club lounge rather than TSA itself (Tue Mar 24, 9:15 AM ET).

The difference between PreCheck and general screening is substantial. Travelers with PreCheck, CLEAR, Real ID Touchless, or Global Entry are moving through in under 5 minutes (Tue Mar 24, 1:36 PM, 1:00 PM, 12:41 PM; Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM and 6:30 PM ET), while standard passengers face 15–25 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM ET). Even during what should be busier periods—late afternoon through early evening—waits remain short (Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM ET).

Recommendation: If you have PreCheck or equivalent programs, arrive 90 minutes before domestic departure. If traveling standard TSA, plan for 2 hours early to allow buffer time. Current conditions are favorable, but standard lines can shift quickly during peak hours, so avoid cutting it close.
