Mar 24: 11:17 PM|8:53 PM|7:24 PM|6:27 PM|5:25 PM|4:27 PM|3:35 PM|3:21 PM|2:34 PM

PreCheck and touchless security at MIA have been moving exceptionally fast. A traveler with touchless PreCheck cleared TSA in 2 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:36 PM), and another reported 3 minutes from drop-off through touchless PreCheck (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:41 PM). A PreCheck holder with Real ID Touchless got through in under 5 minutes at 6:30 PM, while the regular line was estimated at 15–20 minutes (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 8:12 PM). CLEAR also performed well: one traveler cleared in 5 minutes while the regular line sat at approximately 25 minutes (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM). General TSA waits without PreCheck or CLEAR have ranged from 6–10 minutes in the morning (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:29 AM) to 15–25 minutes depending on time of day.

One report noted Checkpoint 2 priority as fast on Thursday night (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 4:51 PM). Another traveler arriving 3 hours early experienced only a 5-minute TSA wait (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 8:12 PM). Global Entry processing took about 20 minutes, though one traveler's experience was slightly extended due to facial recognition updates for a family member (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 3:27 PM).

Recommendation: Arrive 1.5–2 hours early for domestic flights if flying standard security; PreCheck or CLEAR holders can arrive 1 hour early, as recent reports show consistent 2–5 minute wait times through those lanes. MIA has been staffed efficiently and moving travelers through quickly, but standard lines can still reach 20–25 minutes during busier periods, so building in buffer time remains prudent.
