PreCheck and touchless options at MIA have been moving quickly. A traveler with touchless PreCheck cleared security in 2 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 1:36 PM), and another with touchless cleared in 3 minutes (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 12:41 PM). A traveler using touchless PreCheck reported clearing in under 5 minutes at 6:30 PM (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 8:12 PM). Another with PreCheck + Real ID Touchless got through in under 5 minutes (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 8:12 PM). CLEAR also performed well: one traveler cleared in 5 minutes with CLEAR (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM).

Regular security lines at MIA have been moderate. The regular line was approximately 15–20 minutes at 6:30 PM (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 8:12 PM), and about 25 minutes in the evening (Twitter - Mon Mar 23, 6:22 PM). One traveler reported a 10-minute wait a few days before Mar 24 (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:36 AM). Another experienced 6 minutes through TSA at 9 AM on Mar 23 (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 8:17 AM). One report noted international connecting flights with American Airlines encountered snaking lines at the checkpoint (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 5:51 AM).

General reports indicate MIA has had short to no lines overall. One traveler reported no lines at TSA on Mar 24 (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 9:15 AM), and the official MIA airport account noted short lines and smooth takeoffs (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 10:27 AM). One report from the return leg of a trip mentioned waiting at the ID check for a couple of minutes while the X-ray cleared (Twitter - Tue Mar 24, 11:52 PM).

For the smoothest experience at MIA, consider arriving 2–3 hours early if you have PreCheck or CLEAR, as these programs have consistently delivered waits of 5 minutes or less. If you're using a regular security line, plan for 15–25 minutes; arriving at least 2–3 hours before departure is recommended. Note that international connecting flights may experience longer waits, so allow extra time if you're connecting domestically from an international arrival.
