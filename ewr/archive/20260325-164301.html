TSA PreCheck and CLEAR lines at Terminal A were moving very efficiently today. A traveler with CLEAR and TSA PreCheck waited just 10 minutes for a 7 a.m. flight (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:33 AM). General Reports (Non-Terminal-Specific)

Overall conditions at EWR today have been mixed. A report at 12:42 p.m. indicated security was taking less than 5 minutes total (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:42 PM). However, an alert at 11:55 a.m. noted that average wait times across all lines were running at 50 minutes, compared to a typical 9 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:55 AM). A dashboard report at 11:23 a.m. indicated that EWR had suspended live wait time reporting, with some cases reaching over 2 hours (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:23 AM). Early morning reports were more positive: one traveler got through in 1 minute for a 6 a.m. flight (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:19 AM), and another noted the Starbucks line was longer than TSA security at 8 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:44 AM). PreCheck specifically was reported as very fast this morning at 8 a.m., with no ICE officers observed (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:43 AM). Another traveler reported 20 minutes at EWR and JFK combined (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:17 PM), while a family member of another traveler found EWR was running normally compared to LGA (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:15 PM).

Recommendation

Conditions at EWR are highly variable today; early morning and mid-morning showed fast throughput, but reports after 11 a.m. suggest significant delays have developed. If you have TSA PreCheck or CLEAR, plan for 10–15 minutes5 minutes. For regular security lines, allow at least 30–50 minutes based on today's reports, especially if traveling mid-day or afternoon. Check real-time updates before heading to the airport, as waits appear to be fluctuating significantly.
