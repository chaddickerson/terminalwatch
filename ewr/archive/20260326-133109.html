Airport Update: EWR

Terminal A

Early this morning today, TSA PreCheck wait times at Terminal A were very short. A traveler with Touchless TSA PreCheck cleared security in under 15 minutes at 4:18 am (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:19 AM), and another reported a short line with TSA PreCheck available (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 4:29 AM). However, conditions varied significantly later in the day. One traveler with both CLEAR and TSA PreCheck waited a full 1 hour to get through security on Wednesday afternoon, arriving at 2:40 pm and clearing at 3:40 pm (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:32 PM). On Wednesday mid-day, a United passenger reported approximately 30 minutes in the regular security line (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 5:11 PM). Very early on Wednesday, Touchless TSA PreCheck was only 5 minutes at 5 am (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 5:00 AM). From earlier in the week, a traveler reported clearing security in just 5 minutes at curb-to-TSA with PreCheck on Monday evening (Bluesky - Mon Mar 23, 6:27 PM).

Terminal B

Today, Terminal B saw particularly light traffic. A Spirit Airlines passenger got through security in just 4 minutes (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:19 AM). On Wednesday evening, another traveler cleared security in 13 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 7:15 PM).

Terminal C

Terminal C has shown mixed conditions today. Very early this morning at 4:56 am, TSA PreCheck lines were moving at a 6–10 minute pace, though the line appeared longer than it actually was (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 5:07 AM). A traveler with both TSA PreCheck and CLEAR cleared in just 8 minutes (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:12 AM). However, standard security (regular line) was running approximately 30 minutes this morning (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:20 AM). On Wednesday evening at 7 pm, PreCheck was fairly empty while regular security lines looked to be around 30 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:33 PM). Earlier in the week on Tuesday morning, security took less than 20 minutes (Bluesky - Tue Mar 24, 10:22 AM). Global Entry / PreCheck holders saw 5-minute lines mid-week (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:57 PM).

General Reports

A traveler with PreCheck arriving at 7 am today reported it took about 10 minutes from the shuttle bus to getting in line for Starbucks inside security, noting there were many lines but manageable with PreCheck (Bluesky - Thu Mar 26, 9:04 AM). Another traveler today noted that while all lines were long, security moved quickly and efficiently (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 7:22 AM). One commenter observed that TSA PreCheck passengers experienced much shorter waits while regular lines "look like a disaster" (Twitter - Thu Mar 26, 6:46 AM). A traveler who flew from multiple airports (ORD, MIA, LGA, and EWR) last week never waited more than 10 minutes at any TSA line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:21 PM).

Recommendation for Arriving Travelers

Terminal B appears to be the fastest option with consistently short wait times. For Terminal C and Terminal A, TSA PreCheck and CLEAR make a significant difference—PreCheck holders can expect 5–15 minutes in early morning hours and 8–30 minutes later in the day, while regular security lines are running 30 minutes or longer. If you have PreCheck or CLEAR, plan to arrive 1.5 hours before departure; without these programs, allow 2.5 hours. Early morning departures (before 6 am) will encounter noticeably shorter lines across all terminals.
