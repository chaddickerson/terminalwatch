Terminal A

Today, PreCheck and CLEAR were offline at Terminal A due to the shutdown (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:18 PM). Earlier today, one traveler with CLEAR and TSA PreCheck waited 1 hour total to get through security (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:32 PM). However, other reports from earlier in the day were more favorable: CLEAR and regular lines were both under 15 minutes at 8:45 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:51 AM), and one traveler got through in just 5 minutes with CLEAR and TSA PreCheck at 7 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:33 AM). PreCheck alone took 5 minutes at 5 a.m. (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 5:00 AM). Mid-day Wednesday, TSA took about 30 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:11 PM). One traveler reported getting through in under 10 minutes with regular security today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:04 PM).

Terminal B

Today at Terminal B, all screening lines were open and wait times were under 30 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:44 PM).

Terminal C

At 7 p.m. today, Terminal C was running normally with PreCheck fairly empty and regular security at approximately 30 minutes (Bluesky - Wed Mar 25, 7:33 PM). Earlier today, PreCheck (Touchless ID) took just 11 minutes at 6:03 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:21 PM). One traveler reported getting through the regular line in 38 seconds at 11 a.m. with virtually no line (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 5:30 PM). PreCheck took just 3 minutes at 9 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:22 AM). Global Entry/PreCheck users reported 5-minute waits (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 3:38 PM).

Reports without terminal-specific information show highly variable conditions today. An automated alert indicated average waits across all terminals reached 83 minutes at 7:12 p.m., though this figure had dropped to 45 minutes by 9:13 p.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:13 PM). Wait times spiked to 1.5 hours today, with lines wrapping around the bag check area (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:59 PM). One traveler flew out today in just 20 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:23 PM). Another traveler reported getting through in under 40 minutes for an afternoon flight this week (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 10:02 PM). One traveler experienced the fastest security time ever: under 5 minutes in the regular line at midday (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:42 PM). Early morning departures saw short waits: 20 minutes at 7 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:08 PM), and TSA PreCheck was empty at 1 p.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:11 PM).

Waits have been highly variable throughout today, ranging from under 5 minutes in some instances to over 1.5 hours in others. If you have TSA PreCheck, use it—several reports show PreCheck lines moving significantly faster than regular lines, with waits of 5–15 minutes compared to 30+ minutes in standard queues. Note that PreCheck and CLEAR were offline at Terminal A for part of today due to the shutdown. Early morning flights (before 8 a.m.) and mid-day departures appear to have experienced shorter waits. Allow 45 minutes to 1 hour for security at Newark today, or more if traveling during peak afternoon hours. If you have PreCheck, you may be able to get through in 10–15 minutes.
