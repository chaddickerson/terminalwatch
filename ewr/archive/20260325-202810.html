General reports (no specific terminal): Today afternoon, a TSA wait time sensor reported average wait times across all lines at 30 minutes, which is 19–21 minutes longer than typical (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:58 PM and 1:56 PM). Earlier today at 11:55 a.m., that same sensor flagged 50-minute waits, 41 minutes above normal (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 11:55 AM). A traveler who flew early this morning reported clearing TSA PreCheck with no line at 6 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 9:19 AM). Another early traveler with CLEAR + TSA Pre took 10 minutes for a 7 a.m. flight (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 6:33 AM). TSA PreCheck was reported as very fast at 8 a.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 8:43 AM). A general report indicated less than 5 minutes overall today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 12:42 PM), and another traveler cleared security in 15 minutes (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:48 PM). TSA PreCheck had no line at all at 1 p.m. (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 1:11 PM). A traveler reported 20 minutes at some point today (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 4:08 PM), and another mentioned 20 minutes after flying out this morning (Twitter - Wed Mar 25, 2:31 PM).

Recommendation: If you have TSA PreCheck or Global Entry, plan to arrive 15–20 minutes before your target security clearance time; Terminal A and Terminal C are both reporting very fast PreCheck and touchless processing. If you're flying regular security, plan an extra 20–30 minutes as afternoon times have been longer than usual today. Early morning flights (before 7 a.m.) have consistently short or no lines across all terminals. CLEAR members can expect the fastest processing in all terminals.
