TSA PreCheck lines tell a different story. Travelers with PreCheck consistently reported much faster processing: 5–7 minutes at Terminal 5 (Mon Mar 23, 1:19 PM and 12:08 PM), and 5–6 minutes at Terminal 2 (Mon Mar 23, 8:36 PM). However, one traveler noted the PreCheck line was notably slower on Tuesday (Tue Mar 24, 1:58 PM), suggesting occasional congestion even in expedited lanes.

Reports from the past several days (Mon Mar 23 through Tue Mar 24) show morning and mid-day times generally running under 20 minutes for standard lines, with morning hours appearing fastest. Earlier data from the weekend (Sat Mar 21 – Sun Mar 22) reported 15-minute waits, though one outlier mentioned a potential 3-hour wait (Sun Mar 22, 11:45 PM)—which appears inconsistent with other concurrent reports and may reflect a specific incident.

Recommendation: Arrive at least 90 minutes before departure for domestic flights if using standard security. If you have TSA PreCheck, 60 minutes should be sufficient based on recent reports. Terminal choice may matter—Terminal 5 has shown particularly quick processing recently, so confirm your airline's terminal before departure.
