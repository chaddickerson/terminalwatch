Earlier reports: Mar 24, 2026 11:17 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 10:40 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:57 AM ET|Mar 24, 2026 09:24 AM ET

Most recent reports from Monday morning (Mon Mar 23, 8:35 AM) indicate TSA lines at O'Hare were basically non-existent, with travelers reporting 5-minute waits through security. Terminal 5 saw particularly fast times, with TSA PreCheck passengers clearing security in 5-7 minutes (Mon Mar 23, 1:19 PM and 12:08 PM). However, an official TSA alert (Mon Mar 23, 8:10 AM) warned that average wait times across all lines were 25 minutes—significantly above the typical 5-minute average—so conditions may vary by terminal and time of day.

TSA PreCheck holders have consistently experienced shorter waits. Reports show PreCheck passengers getting through in 5-20 minutes across multiple terminals, while general security line data is more limited. Clear members combined with PreCheck had near-instant processing (Mon Mar 23, 10:01 AM). One traveler reported a 20-minute wait through general TSA lines (Mon Mar 23, 10:48 AM), suggesting general security may run longer than PreCheck lines.

Early morning appears to be the fastest window based on reports from Sunday and Monday mornings (Sun Mar 22 – Mon Mar 23, 8-10 AM), with multiple travelers noting minimal or no waits. Evening travel showed mixed results, with one TSA PreCheck passenger reporting less than 7 minutes at 6:40 PM (Mon Mar 23, 12:08 PM).

Recommendation: Arrive 45 minutes to 1 hour before your domestic flight to account for potential 25-minute security waits, longer if traveling during peak afternoon hours. If you have TSA PreCheck or Clear, you can reduce this to 30-45 minutes. Early morning (before 10 AM) appears to offer the fastest experience based on recent reports.
